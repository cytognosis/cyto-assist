### We're in the elevator for 30 seconds. Give us your pitch. *

Cytognosis builds AI-powered noninvasive sensors that detect cellular disease signatures before people become patients.

To achieve this, we're building two synergistic components:

1. Multimodal AI maps early cellular/molecular disease drivers (think RDoC, not DSM - biology, not just symptoms)

The moonshot: Catch disease 5-10 years earlier when prevention actually works, making it accessible to everyone through noninvasive monitoring

## Technology

> [!NOTE]
> Please only enter non-confidential information. If you are connected to mentors, they will sign NDAs.

### What is the problem you are solving or the need you are meeting? *

To both identify early cellular signatures and noninvasive sensors to monitor them to allow early cellular diagnostics ("Cytognosis") years before clinical symptoms emerge.

### Who is your customer? How do you know they need your technology? *

Everyone—before they become patients.
We're not targeting diseased populations; we're catching warning signs in healthy people when they can easily be addressed, before disease defines their identity. By the time someone is an "Alzheimer's patient," they've had years of brain damage. We detect cellular dysfunction years earlier when prevention works.

### What is your solution? *

We are building technologies for mapping (Cytoverse) and tracking (Cytoscope) of health states. On the former side, we are building multimodal foundation models, using many of the recent single-cell atlases that we have been leading in the past few years. The second part, however, is much less behind as even if we have the best markers, if we can't measure them noninvasively, we haven't really solved any problems. Recent emergence of PET tracer imaging and MRS technologies that allow measurement of specific molecular readouts being unique hopes for creating new datasets that allows us to bridge noninvasive imaging modalities with detailed molecular readouts.
