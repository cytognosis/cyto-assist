## What is Convergent Research?

Convergent Research is a nonprofit "venture creation studio" that identifies scientific bottlenecks through systematic technical roadmapping with world-leading scientists, then incubates purpose-built Focused Research Organizations (FROs) to solve them. They act as mission control: providing operational, financial, and legal support to subsidiary FROs that function as nonprofit startups.

Convergent does NOT directly fund FROs. Instead, they:

1. Identify bottlenecks via their [Gap Map](https://www.essentialtechnology.blog/p/introducing-the-convergent-research)
2. Find and coach founding teams
3. Develop extensive technical proposals (60+ page documents reviewed by world experts)
4. Connect FROs with their funder network

**Typical FRO profile**: $20-50M initial grants, 3-7 year missions, full-time teams at industry wages, clear milestones, all outputs as public goods.

## 1-2 Page Proposal Structure

Based on analysis of the FAS Scalable Molecular Monitoring proposal, Convergent's stated evaluation criteria, and their common mistakes guidance, the optimal structure for the 1-2 page PDF is:

### Recommended Template

```
TITLE: [Clear, descriptive, no jargon]

1. THE GAP (1/4 page)
   - What critical scientific infrastructure is missing?
   - Why can't academia, startups, or national labs build it?
   - What is the specific bottleneck blocking progress?

2. PROJECT CONCEPT (1/2 page)
   - What will you build? (concrete deliverables, not aspirations)
   - Technical approach in 3-4 sentences
   - Stage-gate milestones (3-4 stages, each with clear deliverable)

3. WHY A FRO? (1/4 page)
   - Why does this require a new nonprofit organization?
   - What non-publishable, engineering-heavy work is needed?
   - Why industry won't do it (market failure argument)
   - What public good does it produce?

4. TEAM AND TIMELINE (brief)
   - Key personnel and their unique qualifications
   - Duration (3-7 years) and approximate budget range
   - Open science commitment: what will be released publicly?
```

### What Makes a Strong FRO Proposal (from Convergent)

**Must demonstrate**:

- 80%+ engineering, 20% research (coordinated effort, not blue-sky exploration)
- Requires hiring non-trainee talent at industry wages
- Significant non-publishable work that academia can't do
- Outputs are public goods (datasets, tools, protocols, reagents)
- Cannot be done as a startup (would curtail/alter mission)
- Unglamorous, infrastructure-type work that is underrated but transformative

### Four Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it fails | Cytognosis avoidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "My lab, but with more money" | Should stay in academia if feasible there | Cytognosis requires industrial-scale screening + engineering |
| "20 great researchers doing blue-sky" | Not coordinated; wastes FRO structure | Cytognosis has clear stage-gate milestones |
| "My startup idea with nonprofit funding" | If viable as startup, use VC instead | Cytognosis produces public goods (open health coordinate system); no single-indication IP play |
| "Tackle the biggest intellectual challenge" | FROs excel at unglamorous tool-building | Cytognosis builds infrastructure (Cytoverse as "AddGene for disease biology") |

## FAS Molecular Monitoring FRO: Parallel Analysis

**Title**: "A Focused Research Organization to Develop a Modular and Scalable Platform for Human Molecular Monitoring"
**Source**: [fas.org/publication/fro-scalable-molecular-monitoring](https://fas.org/publication/fro-scalable-molecular-monitoring/)

### Project Concept

Goal: Achieve minimally invasive continuous monitoring of 100+ analytes in the human body and deliver devices to researchers. Technical approach: synthetic ion channels + conformational switches + CMOS array for parallel multi-analyte sensing.

### Stage-Gate Structure (Model for Cytognosis)

| Stage | FAS Molecular Monitoring | Cytognosis Analog |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Survey & Prioritize** | Survey metabolic, hormone, immune targets for greatest explanatory power | Survey cellular biomarkers, disease axes, intervention targets for greatest health trajectory predictive power |
| **2. Develop Platform** | Integrated circuit with biosensors in wearable form factor; validate against blood samples | Cytoverse AI coordinate system + Cytoscope biosensor platform; validate against clinical diagnostics |
| **3. Distribute Devices** | Share validated devices in bulk to catalyze field research | Open-source Cytoverse models + Cytoscope reference designs to research community |
| **4. Curate Atlas** | Time-varying human metabolome database | Time-varying cellular health atlas (continuous spectrum mapping) |

### Why FRO Justification (Their Model)

The FAS proposal makes three structural arguments that map precisely to Cytognosis:

| Their Argument | Our Version |
| --- | --- |
| "Medical device industry focuses on handful of molecules (diabetes)" | "Pharma focuses on late-stage, patentable compounds, not disease biology validation" |
| "Nonprofit institute best suited to standardize and derisk" | "Cytognosis as public utility for disease biology, like AddGene for genetic material" |
| "Four stages require tight feedback loops and standardization" | "Our three-pillar platform requires coordinated engineering across AI, biosensing, and edge computing" |
| "Like AddGene democratized access to genetic material" | "Like AddGene, Cytognosis democratizes access to validated disease biology" |

### Key Structural Parallel

Both proposals share the same core narrative arc:

1. **Current state**: Monitoring exists for one molecule (glucose) / Disease biology validation is fragmented
2. **The gap**: No platform exists for broad, continuous monitoring / No systematic approach to target validation
3. **Why FRO**: Industry incentives misaligned (diabetes dominates / patent system rewards compounds not biology)
4. **The deliverable**: Open-access platform + atlas (metabolome database / cellular health coordinate system)

## Cytognosis FRO Proposal: Draft Elements

### Proposed Title

"A Focused Research Organization to Build an Open Cellular Intelligence Platform for Proactive Health"

### 1-Line Summary

"Cytognosis will build the first open-source cellular intelligence platform that maps personalized health states along continuous biological axes, detecting and intercepting disease years before symptoms emerge."

### Keywords

AI, Biology/Biotechnology, Genomics, Global Health, Health and Aging, Medicine, Neuroscience

### The Gap

Healthcare operates with three blindspots that existing institutions cannot address:

1. **Mechanistic blindspot**: Current diagnostics measure symptoms, not causes. ICD codes create artificial disease silos that blind researchers to shared biological mechanisms across disorders.
2. **Temporal blindspot**: By the time clinical symptoms trigger diagnosis, significant organ damage is irreversible. No continuous monitoring platform exists that tracks pre-symptomatic disease trajectories.
3. **Complexity blindspot**: Population averages mask individual variation. No existing system maps personalized health states along continuous biological axes.

Academia cannot build this because it requires coordinated engineering across AI, biosensing, and edge computing at industrial scale, with significant non-publishable integration work. Startups cannot build this because the outputs must be open public goods; a single-indication diagnostic startup would necessarily narrow scope to achieve VC returns.

### Project Concept (4-Stage)

1. **Map** (Year 1-2): Build Cytoverse, an AI health coordinate system that maps disease biology along continuous axes (inflammation, proteostasis, metabolic flux), trained on multi-omic data. Release as open-access reference atlas.
2. **Sense** (Year 2-4): Develop Cytoscope, a programmable continuous biosensing platform capable of monitoring 50+ cellular analytes. Validate against clinical gold standards.
3. **Navigate** (Year 3-5): Deploy Cytonome, an on-device causal AI engine (<5mW) that runs personalized health trajectory predictions locally, preserving privacy.
4. **Distribute** (Year 4-7): Share validated platform to research community. Curate time-varying cellular health atlas. Enable population-scale studies that were previously impossible.

### Why a FRO?

Cytognosis requires a FRO because it sits in the structural gap between academia and industry:

- **Not academic**: Requires 30+ engineers at industry wages, industrial-grade manufacturing, and years of non-publishable integration work
- **Not a startup**: Core outputs (health coordinate system, validated disease biology) must be open public goods; restricting access would undermine the mission
- **Not a national lab**: Requires startup-like speed, flat hierarchy, and tight feedback loops across AI, biology, and hardware

Cytognosis is the "AddGene for disease biology": a centralized, nonprofit infrastructure that de-risks the public good of target validation. Just as AddGene democratized access to genetic material, Cytognosis will democratize access to continuous cellular health monitoring.

### Shareable Information (for funders)

"Cytognosis Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit (EIN 39-4383634) building an open cellular intelligence platform. Our three-pillar architecture (Cytoverse AI coordinate system, Cytoscope continuous biosensor, Cytonome on-device causal AI) addresses three structural blindspots in healthcare: mechanistic, temporal, and complexity. The founding team has 20+ years experience in computational biology (MIT, Broad Institute, insitro, GenBio AI). We seek $20-40M over 5-7 years to produce open-access tools, validated disease biology atlases, and reference biosensor designs as public goods."

### FRO Outputs (What Convergent Expects)

All FRO outputs must be public goods:

- Open-source or open-access datasets and protocols
- Reagents or tools distributed to researchers
- Research papers
- Educational resources, workshops, visitor programs
- Integration into larger academic/philanthropic/government projects
- IP licensing to companies (only when necessary for maximal public benefit)
- Spin out new entities providing ongoing community services
