## Organization Overview

**Astera Institute** is a 501(c)(3) philanthropic foundation (like Cytognosis) on a mission to help lead humanity's transition to a scientifically advanced, abundant future. Founded by Jed McCaleb. Headquarters in Emeryville, California. Supports creation of public goods for science and technology.

### Two Major Programs

#### 1. Neuro & AGI Program

A directed neuroscience and AI research effort, seeking to understand how biological intelligence works to aid efforts to engineer safe and successful general intelligence systems. Three sub-areas:

**Neuroscience**: Decoding how patterns of neural activity across 87 billion neurons become thoughts, perceptions, and memories that constitute conscious experience. Built on the hypothesis that the brain uses compositional architecture, combining elemental units through neural syntax. Aims to transition neuroscience from observation to engineering by reading from and writing to neural circuits at scale.

Key research questions:

- How the brain represents and manipulates objects and concepts
- What neural circuitry gives rise to consciousness
- Better techniques to read and write from the brain
- Ways to merge and upload consciousness

**Neuro-AI**: A radical departure from the "scale is all you need" philosophy. Turns to neuroscience to decode the biological blueprints of the mind. Investigates how natural systems achieve profound cognition on the power consumption of a lightbulb. Prioritizes discovery of fundamental algorithmic principles that might lead to efficient, elegant intelligence.

Key research questions:

- How does an agent learn from experience and continuously adapt to a changing environment?
- Alternative architectures beyond compute scaling

**AI Safety**: Ensuring safe and successful general intelligence systems.

#### 2. Foundation Program

Centralized operational and financial support. Leads the Residency Program, supports Fellows, explores new opportunities, and drives ecosystem-supporting efforts.

- **Strategic Investments**: Portfolio of ventures and organizations
- **Residency**: 12-18 month fully funded program (see below)
- **Fellows**: Individual support

## Recent Strategic Shift (2025-2026)

Astera has significantly evolved its focus areas. Previously centered on Open Science and general technology public goods, the institute has made a major strategic pivot to prioritize:

1. **Neuroscience** as a core programmatic focus (new dedicated landing page, dedicated research team)
2. **Neuro-AI** as a distinct research area (alternative to scale-is-all-you-need, biologically inspired AI)
3. **AI Safety** as a distinct concern area
4. **Life Sciences** broadened from just open science infrastructure

This shift positions neuroscience and artificial intelligence as co-equal with the original open science mission. The residency tracks now explicitly lead with "Neuroscience and Artificial Intelligence" as Track 1.

### Three Tracks

#### Track 1: Neuroscience and Artificial Intelligence ★

Projects at the interface of neuroscience and AI that deepen understanding of the biological brain and enable more powerful, robust, and interpretable artificial systems.

**Foundational frameworks:**

- Mathematical frameworks for learning beyond gradient descent in feedback-driven systems
- Computational frameworks for large-scale neuroscience datasets
- Representation/structure learning for causal inference

**Brain modeling:**

- Bottom-up brain modeling grounded in connectomics and biological plasticity rules
- Neurally inspired models of perception or intelligence

**Experimental innovations:**

- New techniques for measuring/manipulating neural circuits at scale
- Human neuroscience experiments to study problem solving
- Novel approaches to understanding the brain's self-model

**Cross-disciplinary integration:**

- Efforts linking neural mechanisms and machine learning architectures

#### Track 2: Life Sciences and Open Science ★★

Projects accelerating progress in life science with clear real-world utility and impact.

- New technologies/platforms for AI-ready missing data generation in life science
- Infrastructure/tools for dynamic, machine-readable, reusable research sharing
- Computational tools for data-driven decision-making in science funding

#### Track 3: Other

Projects that align with Astera's mission but do not fit neatly into above tracks.

### Application Process (6 Stages)

1. **Initial Application**: Basic info + 2-min video + 8 questions (each ≤1000 chars)
2. **Introductory Conversation**: 15-25 min call with Astera team
3. **Detailed Project Proposal**: Full proposal with preliminary budget and milestones
4. **Executive Conversations**: Scope, budget, and milestone alignment
5. **References**
6. **Offers Extended** (early June 2026)

### Who Should Apply

Entrepreneurs, engineers, industry scientists, promising academics. Co-founders welcome. Non-dilutive funding for open projects (one-off public good or precursor to future org).

### Open Science Expectations

Residents are expected to publish openly and often, producing accessible outputs and sharing learnings, including what did not work. However, terms can be negotiated for projects with potential commercial interests.
