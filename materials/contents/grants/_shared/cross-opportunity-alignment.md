## Reproducible Language Blocks

These are funder-agnostic core statements that can be adapted for any proposal. Each block addresses a common evaluation criterion.

### Block 1: The Problem (3 Blindspots)

> Healthcare operates with three structural blindspots. The **mechanistic blindspot**: current diagnostics measure symptoms, not causes, creating artificial disease silos through ICD codes that blind researchers to shared biological mechanisms. The **temporal blindspot**: by the time clinical symptoms trigger diagnosis, significant organ damage is typically irreversible, yet no continuous monitoring platform tracks pre-symptomatic disease trajectories. The **complexity blindspot**: population averages mask individual variation, and no existing system maps personalized health states along continuous biological axes.

**Use in**: All proposals. Adapt length: 1 sentence (pitch), 1 paragraph (abstract), 3 paragraphs (full proposal).

### Block 2: The Platform (GPS for Health)

> Cytognosis builds the "GPS for Health": three integrated pillars that together enable proactive healthspan preservation. **Cytoverse** (The Map) constructs an AI health coordinate system that maps disease biology along continuous axes, trained on multi-omic data. **Cytoscope** (The Sensor) provides programmable continuous biosensing, monitoring cellular analytes in real time. **Cytonome** (The Navigator) runs on-device causal AI at less than 5 milliwatts, computing personalized health trajectory predictions locally to preserve privacy.

**Use in**: All proposals. For technical funders (NIH, ARPA-H), expand each pillar. For impact funders (DRK, EA), focus on outcomes.

### Block 3: Why FRO (Structural Justification)

> Cytognosis occupies the structural gap between academia (which rewards novel publications over robust standardization) and the for-profit sector (which requires patent protection and rapid ROI). Target identification accounts for only 3-7% of pharmaceutical R&D costs yet governs over 90% of downstream attrition risk. The average discovery-to-approval timeline is 32 years, fundamentally incompatible with 10-year venture fund lifecycles. Cytognosis institutionalizes the synergy that currently forms only through ad hoc partnerships, housing both deep biological exploration and high-throughput screening under one roof.

**Use in**: Convergent, NSF Tech Labs, ARIA FROST UK, Wellcome Leap, Foresight, EA LTFF. Omit for YC/corporate.

### Block 4: Open Science Commitment

> All Cytognosis outputs are released under permissive open licenses: Apache 2.0 for software, CC BY 4.0 for data and publications. AI models are truly open (weights, training data, code, logs, and evaluation, following the Allen AI standard). Data follows FAIR principles with persistent identifiers, machine-readable metadata, open protocols, and standard ontologies. Our open licensing is irrevocable: once released, outputs remain permanently accessible as public infrastructure.

**Use in**: NSF Tech Labs, Convergent, Foresight, ARPA-H (data sharing plan), Horizon Europe, EA LTFF, Anthropic.

### Block 5: Founder Narrative

> Shahin Mohammadi spent 37 years navigating a diagnostic odyssey across specialties, each treating symptoms in isolation. The answer, an ultra-rare TBX1 mutation, was hiding in plain sight in publicly available genomic databases. Mohammadi found it through self-directed genomic analysis, bringing 20 years of computational biology expertise from MIT, the Broad Institute, insitro, and GenBio AI to bear on his own biology. Cytognosis exists so no one else waits decades for answers.

**Use in**: YC, Emergent Ventures, DRK, Astera, Brains (personal story sections). Reference in all others for mission credibility.

### Block 6: Health Equity

> Precision health is a human right, not a privilege. Cytognosis designs for accessibility from line one of code: on-device AI eliminates cloud dependency for underserved communities, open-source licensing removes vendor lock-in for health systems in low-resource settings, and continuous monitoring replaces expensive episodic testing. The platform targets accessibility for at least 75% of communities, with cost targets below $100 per assessment.

**Use in**: ARPA-H (PHO requires ≥75%), NSF (Broader Impacts), Wellcome Leap, DRK, EA LTFF. Adapt cost targets per funder.

### Block 7: Impact Metrics

> Cytognosis measures success by health outcomes changed, not papers published or patents filed. Primary metrics: (1) diseases intercepted years before symptoms emerge, (2) individuals empowered with health autonomy, (3) open tools actively used by the research community (compound impact), (4) cost per quality-adjusted life year gained versus current standard of care.

**Use in**: All impact-oriented funders (DRK, EA, Wellcome). For ARPA-H/NIH, translate to clinical endpoints.

### Block 8: The Helix Architecture / 15-Year Lifecycle

> Healthcare innovation routinely fails at the phase transition: a 5-year grant ends before a diagnostic reaches clinical utility, leaving the technology stranded in the "Valley of Death." Cytognosis operationalizes the Helix Framework, a 15-year, three-stage lifecycle designed for continuity. Years 1-5 utilize philanthropic R&D to solve critical bottlenecks; Years 6-10 leverage catalytic capital to de-risk clinical and regulatory pathways; Years 11-15 raise commercial funding for global deployment. This ensures that the foundational science has a structural bridge to global scale.

**Use in**: ARPA-H (Transition plans), Wellcome Leap, Convergent Research, EA LTFF, DRK Foundation.

### Block 9: The People's Equity (Mission vs. Dilution)

> In traditional startups, solving early technical risk requires trading ~60% of the company's equity to financial investors by Series B. This "investor premium" forces a commercial mandate that often sacrifices equitable global access. Because Cytognosis funds its early, high-risk R&D non-dilutively through the nonprofit foundation, that 60% equity remains intact. When spinning out commercial subsidiaries for manufacturing and deployment, the operational team receives competitive market-rate equity (~40%) to attract world-class engineering talent. The remaining 60%—"The People's Equity"—is permanently held by the foundation, creating a regenerative engine where commercial success funds tiered global pricing and future open science.

**Use in**: Convergent / FROs, Astera, EA Funds, ARPA-H (Commercialization strategy), YC (Why non-profit).
