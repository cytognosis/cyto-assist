# Other Transactions (OT) Mechanism Reference

A consolidated reference for the Other Transaction funding mechanism used by ARPA-H and NSF Tech Labs.

## What Are Other Transactions?

Other Transactions (OTs) are legally binding instruments used by federal agencies that fall outside the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) and standard grants/cooperative agreements. They provide:

- **Flexibility**: Reduced administrative burden compared to traditional contracts or grants
- **Milestone-based payments**: Funding tied to technical achievement, not cost reimbursement
- **Broader eligibility**: Nonprofits, for-profits, academia, consortia, and non-traditional performers
- **Negotiable IP terms**: IP rights are negotiated per-agreement rather than following FAR defaults
- **Operational autonomy**: Less prescriptive oversight of how work is performed

## Legal Authority

| Agency | Authority | Statute |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| ARPA-H | OT for Research | 42 USC §289 (PHSA) |
| NSF | OT Authority | CHIPS and Science Act §10391 |
| DARPA/DoD | OT for Research/Prototype | 10 USC §4022 |

## ARPA-H OT Structure (from PROSPR Model OT)

### Agreement Structure

- **Articles**: Numbered clauses covering scope, payments, IP, personnel, compliance
- **Attachments**: SOW, Schedule of Milestones and Payments, Deliverable Descriptions, Background IP Table, Quarterly Status Report templates

### Key Articles

| Article | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Scope & Period | Project/agreement period, phases, durations |
| Payments | Milestone-based. Payments tied to deliverable completion, not time elapsed |
| IP Rights | Data rights with three categories: Unlimited, Limited, Restricted (see IP table) |
| Key Personnel | Named individuals; 14 business days notice for changes |
| Human Subjects | Full 45 CFR Part 46 and 21 CFR compliance required |
| Animal Research | USDA registration, PHS Policy, OLAW assurance required |
| Security/HIPAA | 45 CFR Part 160/164 compliance for clinical data |
| Deliverables | Commercialization Plan, IP Success Framework, Market Landscape, DMSP, Milestone Reports, Final Report |

### Deliverable Types

1. **Commercialization Plan**: Path to market, IP landscape, licensing strategy
2. **IP Success Framework**: Patent holders, expiration dates, sublicensing plans
3. **Market Landscape Analysis**: Competitors, market segments, quantitative sizing
4. **Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)**: Aligned with NIH DMSP template
5. **Milestone Reports**: Documentation proving milestone achievement
6. **Property Report**: Items >$20,000 procured with ARPA-H funding

### IP Rights Categories

| Category | Government Access | Performer Rights |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Unlimited** | Full use, modification, distribution | Retains rights |
| **Limited** | Use for government purposes only | Controls commercial use |
| **Restricted** | Minimum rights specified in agreement | Maximum control |

### Compliance Requirements

- Organizational Conflict of Interest (OCI) disclosure (FAR subpart 9.5 equivalent)
- No concurrent professional support services + technical performance
- Prohibition on certain telecommunications (Section 889)
- Research misconduct reporting (42 CFR 93)
- Force Majeure provisions
- Survival clauses for IP, disputes, applicable law

## NSF Tech Labs OT Structure

### Key Differences from ARPA-H

| Aspect | ARPA-H OT | NSF Tech Labs OT |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Funding scale** | Program-specific, varies | $10-50M per team per year |
| **Duration** | Program-specific (often 5 years) | Phase 0 (9mo) + Phase 1 (24mo) + Phase 2 (24+mo) |
| **Team size** | Determined by proposal | 10-50 members expected by Phase 1 |
| **Entity requirement** | Flexible | Must operate as own entity by Phase 1 |
| **IP management** | Negotiated per agreement | IP Management Plan required in Phase 0 |
| **Selection process** | Solution Summary → Full Proposal | 5-8 page abstract → Oral pitch → Phase awards |

### NSF OT Key Principles (from OT Arrangements Guide)

1. **Reduced administrative burden**: No FAR compliance required
2. **Flexibility in cost sharing**: Government and performer cost-sharing ratios negotiable
3. **Performance-based**: Milestones with clear metrics for technical progress
4. **Research security**: Compliance with CHIPS Act research security provisions
5. **Result ownership**: Negotiated case-by-case, not predetermined

## Implications for Cytognosis

As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, Cytognosis is eligible as a performer under both ARPA-H and NSF OTs. Key advantages:

- **No cost-sharing requirement** (nonprofits often exempt from mandatory cost share)
- **OTs accommodate FRO structure**: The OT mechanism was designed for organizations outside traditional academia/industry
- **IP flexibility**: Can negotiate to keep core platform open-source while protecting specific innovations
- **Milestone alignment**: OT milestone structure aligns with Cytognosis phase-gated development approach
