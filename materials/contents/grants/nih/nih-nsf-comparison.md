# NIH & NSF Proposals

## NIH R01 (Major Research Project)

1. **Specific Aims** (1 page) — problem, gap, hypothesis, aims, impact
2. **Significance** (2-3 pages) — importance, knowledge gap, how this fills it
3. **Innovation** (1 page) — conceptual, technical, methodological novelty
4. **Approach** (6-8 pages) — experimental design per aim, alternatives, timeline
5. **Biosketch** — PI and key personnel (NIH format)
6. **Budget** — modular ($250K/yr cap) or detailed justification

## NIH R21 (Exploratory/Developmental)

1. **Specific Aims** (1 page)
2. **Research Strategy** (6 pages combined significance + approach)
3. No preliminary data required (but helpful)
4. Budget: $275K total over 2 years

## NIH Review Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Key Question |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Significance | High | Does it address an important problem? |
| Investigators | Med | Are they qualified? |
| Innovation | Med | Does it challenge existing paradigms? |
| Approach | High | Is the design rigorous and feasible? |
| Environment | Low | Are the resources adequate? |

## NSF Standard Structure

1. **Project Summary** (1 page) — overview, intellectual merit, broader impacts
2. **Project Description** (15 pages) — proposed research plan
3. **References Cited**
4. **Biographical Sketch** — 3 pages per senior person
5. **Budget** — with justification
6. **Facilities & Equipment**
