## Organization Overview

**Coefficient Giving** (rebranded from Open Philanthropy in 2025) is one of the largest philanthropic funders in effective altruism, having deployed **$4B+ in grants since 2014**. Core funder: Good Ventures (Dustin Moskovitz and Cari Tuna). San Francisco-based.

### Core Principles

1. **Strategic Cause Selection**: ITN Framework (Importance, Neglectedness, Tractability). 200+ causes evaluated to identify where philanthropic capital gets the most leverage.
2. **Worldview Diversification**: Splits resources across multiple approaches (near-term proven interventions AND long-term speculative bets), because there is no single "right answer" about what does the most good.
3. **Hits-Based Giving**: Like venture capital. Expects a small number of hits to account for most impact. Willing to fund high-risk bets if potential impact is large enough. "A single breakthrough grant can offset the costs of many unsuccessful efforts."
4. **Cost-Effectiveness**: Estimates expected impact per dollar. Quantitative models where possible, qualitative reasoning where harder to measure.

### Cytognosis Pitch Angle: Science & Global Health R&D

**Frame**: Cytoverse as a **Transformative Science** platform technology that creates an entirely new capability (cellular intelligence) enabling early disease detection across multiple disease categories, analogous to how David Baker's computational protein design platform created new capability for vaccine design.

**ITN framing**:

- **Importance**: Chronic diseases kill 41M people/year; late diagnosis is the central failure mode
- **Neglectedness**: No existing platform integrates multi-omic single-cell data with temporal health state prediction at individual level; current AI health focuses on imaging/EHR, not cellular biology
- **Tractability**: Foundation models for biological data are mature; single-cell atlases exist; edge AI enables on-device inference; what's missing is the integration layer (Cytoverse)

**Hits-based framing**: Cytoverse is a high-risk, high-reward platform bet. If successful, it creates a "PCR for health states," unlocking an entirely new sector of continuous cellular health monitoring. The failure modes are well-defined (insufficient data quality, model generalization, biosensor miniaturization) and each is independently solvable.

## Biosecurity & Pandemic Preparedness

### Focus

Building resilience against biological risk. Includes pathogen surveillance, early warning systems, biodefense infrastructure.

### Cytognosis Fit

Medium-High. Cytoscope continuous biosensing and Cytoverse health state mapping could serve as infrastructure for early pandemic detection at the individual level. The "canary in the coal mine" framing: detecting immune signature shifts before clinical presentation.

## Navigating Transformative AI

### Focus

Ensuring AI is safe and well-governed. Funds technical AI safety research, governance, and capacity building.

### Cytognosis Fit

Medium. Cytonome on-device causal AI (<5mW) aligns with safe AI deployment. Health AI governance arguments. However, this fund focuses primarily on existential risk from AI, not health AI applications.

## Coefficient Language Guide

When writing to Coefficient, use these terms and framings:

| Coefficient Term | How Cytognosis Maps |
| ---------------- | ------------------- |
| "Important, neglected, and tractable" | Map every proposal to the ITN framework explicitly |
| "Hits-based" | Frame as high-risk, high-reward platform bet |
| "Cost-effectiveness" | Estimate QALYs per dollar or lives saved per dollar |
| "Counterfactual" | Emphasize what wouldn't exist without this specific funding |
| "Worldview diversification" | Cytognosis spans near-term (Cytoscope diagnostics) and long-term (Cytoverse platform) |
| "Platform technology" | Use the Baker/protein design analogy |
| "Transformative" | Not incremental improvement; creates new capability class |
| "Neglected" | No one else is building this specific integration |
| "Room for more funding" | Pre-revenue, pre-VC, fills the valley of death |
