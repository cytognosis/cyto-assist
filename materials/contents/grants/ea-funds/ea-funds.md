## What Is Effective Altruism?

Effective Altruism (EA) is a philosophical and social movement that uses evidence and reason to determine the most effective ways to benefit others. Core ideas:

- **Scope sensitivity**: Impact varies enormously between interventions; some do 100x more good per dollar
- **Expected value**: Accept high-risk bets if expected value is high enough
- **Counterfactual reasoning**: Focus on what wouldn't happen without your specific contribution
- **Cause-neutrality**: Choose causes based on impact data, not emotional attachment
- **Moral cosmopolitanism**: All lives matter equally regardless of geography or species
- **Longtermism**: Future people matter; existential risk reduction has outsized expected value

### EA Language and Framing (Critical for Applications)

| EA Term | Definition | Cytognosis Mapping |
| ------- | ---------- | ------------------ |
| **ITN Framework** | Importance, Neglectedness, Tractability. The standard filter for cause selection. | Frame Cytognosis through each dimension explicitly |
| **Expected value** | Probability × impact. Accept high failure rates if upside is enormous. | Cytoverse has small probability of full success but massive global health upside |
| **Counterfactual impact** | What changes because of this specific action that wouldn't happen otherwise? | No one else is building disease-agnostic cellular intelligence platform |
| **Room for more funding** | Can this project absorb more money productively? | Pre-revenue, pre-VC, extensive R&D roadmap |
| **Neglectedness** | How much attention/resources does this already get? | Continuous cellular health monitoring: <$50M/yr globally vs $200B+ in healthcare AI |
| **Hits-based** | Like VC; expect most bets to fail, a few to transform | Frame as a high-upside platform bet |
| **QALYs / DALYs** | Quality/Disability Adjusted Life Years. Standard impact metric. | Estimate QALYs from early disease detection at population scale |
| **Scale** | How many people affected and how deeply? | 41M NCD deaths/yr; 80% of adults 65+ have chronic conditions |
| **Existential risk** (x-risk) | Risks that could permanently curtail humanity's potential | Pandemic biosurveillance (Long-Term Future angle) |
| **Longtermism** | Prioritizing the long-run future because future people matter | Platform infrastructure that serves billions for decades |
| **Marginal impact** | Impact of the next dollar/hour, not the average | Where is the marginal dollar most productive right now? |
| **Cost-effectiveness analysis** | Rigorous quantitative comparison of impact per dollar | Provide explicit estimates, even rough, with uncertainty bounds |
| **Leverage** | Ability to influence more resources than you directly deploy | Open-source platform creates leverage: others build on Cytoverse |

## EA Funds Overview

**EA Funds** is a grantmaking organization operated by Effective Ventures (under the Centre for Effective Altruism). Pools donor capital and distributes it through expert grantmakers focused on causes identified as the highest-impact by EA reasoning.

### Key Features

- **Expert grantmakers**: Subject-matter specialists with grantmaking experience
- **Counterfactual focus**: Explicitly considers what other funders are doing
- **Single desk**: One application point for emerging projects
- **Quarterly grant rounds**: Regular application cycles
- **Coordination with other funders**: Avoids duplication, fills funding gaps
- **Rolling applications**: Apply anytime via [funds.effectivealtruism.org/apply-for-funding](https://funds.effectivealtruism.org/apply-for-funding)

## Long-Term Future Fund ⭐ (Highest Priority)

### Focus Areas

1. **Projects reducing existential risks** through technical research, policy analysis, advocacy, and demonstration projects
2. **Training and recruitment** for researchers working on existential risk mitigation
3. **Infrastructure** for people working on longtermist projects
4. **Promoting long-term thinking**

### Grant Types

- Individual researcher support (most common)
- Organizational grants for AI safety, biosecurity infrastructure
- Training programs, community building
- **Grant sizes**: Typically $10K-$500K (smaller than Coefficient, larger than GiveDirectly)

### Recent Featured Grants

- **Logan Smith**: AI safety research
- **Robert Miles**: AI safety public communication
- **Jeffrey Ladish**: Security research
- **Sage Bergerson**: AI alignment
- Biosecurity summit funding
- Digital infrastructure for AI safety ecosystem

### Why It Fits Cytognosis

**Biosecurity angle**: Cytoverse and Cytoscope provide continuous population-level health monitoring that could detect emerging pathogens at the individual cellular level before clinical presentation. This is a fundamentally different approach to pandemic preparedness versus traditional surveillance (wastewater, syndromic).

**Longtermist argument**: A cellular intelligence platform that operates at the biological level (not symptom level) creates infrastructure that protects against known AND unknown future health threats, including novel pathogens, engineered bioweapons, and slow-onset diseases.

**HOWEVER**: This fund primarily focuses on AI existential risk (Skynet-style scenarios) and biosecurity policy. Health monitoring technology is adjacent but not core. The pitch would need to emphasize the **existential risk reduction** component (pandemic detection) rather than the health/wellness component.

### Application

- Rolling via [Paperform](https://av20jp3z.paperform.co/?fund=Long-Term%20Future%20Fund)
- Quarterly disbursement cycles
- Contact: <funds@effectivealtruism.org>

## Global Health and Development Fund

### Focus Areas

- Directly provide healthcare or preventive measures improving health, wellbeing, or life expectancy
- Directly provide services raising incomes or improving economic conditions
- Assist governments in designing effective policies

### Management

**Managed by GiveWell** (Elie Hassenfeld, CEO). This means the fund follows GiveWell's rigorous cost-effectiveness methodology.

### Grant Examples

- Malaria Consortium (chemoprevention)
- Johns Hopkins University (impact research)
- Center for Global Development (policy)
- Against Malaria Foundation (bed nets)
- One Acre Fund (agricultural income)

### Why It (Partially) Fits Cytognosis

This fund targets **proven, evidence-backed interventions** in low-income settings. Cytognosis would fit ONLY if:

- Cytoscope devices could be positioned as **point-of-care diagnostics for LMICs**
- Clear cost-effectiveness data exists (QALYs/dollar vs. existing interventions)
- The technology is near-deployment, not R&D stage

**HONEST ASSESSMENT**: This is NOT a fit right now. GiveWell's standard is extremely high for cost-effectiveness evidence. Cytognosis is pre-revenue R&D. Come back when there's a deployable diagnostic tool with cost-effectiveness data.

### Application Language Guide

**For Coefficient** (more formal, research-oriented):

- Lead with ITN analysis
- Provide cost-effectiveness estimates (even rough)
- Reference David Baker / transformative science precedent
- Emphasize platform nature and multiplier effects
- Show how this creates a new capability class
- Frame open-source commitment as maximizing impact per philanthropic dollar

**For EA Funds** (more community-oriented, impact-maximizing):

- Lead with expected value calculation
- Emphasize counterfactual: "Without this specific funding, no one builds cellular intelligence for health"
- Use scale framing: "41M deaths/year from NCDs, each preceded by years of detectable biological drift"
- Cite neglectedness: total global investment in continuous cellular monitoring < $50M/year
- Provide concrete room for more funding estimates
- For Long-Term Future: frame as pandemic detection infrastructure (x-risk reduction)
- Use EA community language: "in expectation," "marginal impact," "room for more funding," "tractability"

### Key Differences From Government Funders

| Dimension | Coefficient / EA Funds | ARPA-H / NSF |
| --------- | ---------------------- | ------------ |
| Selection framework | ITN + expected value + counterfactual | Technical merit + innovation + team |
| Risk tolerance | Very high (hits-based) | High (ARPA-H), Medium (NSF) |
| Grant size | $50K to $10M+ | $10M to $50M+ |
| Timeline | 1-5 years, flexible | 3-5 years, milestone-based |
| Reporting | Light, impact-focused | Heavy, compliance-focused |
| IP | Generally open | OT-specific or Government Rights |
| Relationship | Proactive outreach, program officer relationships | Competitive solicitation |
| Application | Rolling / relationship-based (Coefficient), quarterly (EA) | Periodic solicitations |
| Key question | "Does this maximize good done per dollar?" | "Does this advance the technical state of the art?" |

## How to Apply

### Coefficient

1. No formal RFP. Grants sourced through proactive research by program officers.
2. Build relationships: Attend EA Global, GHD conferences, reach out directly.
3. Contact: [coefficientgiving.org/about-us/partner-with-us/](https://coefficientgiving.org/about-us/partner-with-us/)
4. Prepare: one-page concept note with ITN analysis, cost-effectiveness estimate, team background, and funding ask

### EA Funds

1. **Apply**: [funds.effectivealtruism.org/apply-for-funding](https://funds.effectivealtruism.org/apply-for-funding)
2. Rolling applications, quarterly disbursement
3. Select the appropriate fund(s) on the form
4. Provide: project description, impact theory, budget, team, counterfactual analysis
5. Contact: <funds@effectivealtruism.org>
