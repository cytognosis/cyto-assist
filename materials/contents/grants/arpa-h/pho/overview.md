# ARPA-H Proactive Health Office (PHO) Guidelines

## Mission Overview
The Proactive Health Office (PHO) targets the development of novel early-detection methods, prophylactic interventions, and system-level innovations to transition the healthcare system from reactive to predictive/preventive.

## Key Interest Areas
1. **Prevention, Detection, Prophylactic Treatments**: Continuous, scalable, low-cost sensing of health-state and early disease indicators.
2. **Population-Level Adoption**: Novel methods to educate and incentivize individuals toward healthy, wellness-promoting behaviors (including mental health resiliency).
3. **System Innovation for Delivery**: Predictive surrogates for long-term health outcomes, epidemiological models, and new funding paradigms for preventive care.

## Exclusions
**Do NOT propose**:
- Evolutionary or incremental advances.
- Clinical trials for otherwise developed products.
- Traditional education, center formation, or physical infrastructure.

## Application Mechanism & Flow
This uses an **Innovative Solutions Opening (ISO)** awarding **Other Transactions (OTs)**.
- **Step 1: Solution Summary**: A short, high-level pitch. Must receive encouraging feedback from ARPA-H before proceeding to Step 2.
- **Step 2: Technical & Management Proposal**: Max 20 pages. Focuses heavily on the technical approach, milestones, and statement of work.

## Evaluation Criteria (Descending Order)
1. **Overall Scientific and Technical Merit**: Is the approach innovative, feasible, and complete? Are risks identified with mitigation plans (Plan B)?
2. **Potential Contribution & Relevance to ARPA-H Mission**: Does it address unmet needs and transform biomedicine? What is the transition strategy to commercialization? Open-source software standards are preferred.
3. **Proposer's Capabilities & Related Experience**: Does the team have the track record to pull off this high-risk endeavor?
4. **Cost/Price/Budget Assessment**: Is the budget reflective of the novel solution and level of effort? (Junior personnel or overly "safe" low-risk budgets are discouraged).

## Strategic Positioning
- Do not frame proposals as "safe." Take huge, calculated risks.
- Emphasize the "What if it works?" payoff.
- OTs give immense flexibility—frame the approach around distinct, measurable milestones that trigger funding phases rather than standard grant years.
