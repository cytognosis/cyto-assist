## Expanded Focus Areas for LLM Consumption

### Primary Target: AI-Driven Omics Platform for Definitive Diagnosis

The HSF ISO seeks "innovative approaches at the intersection of artificial intelligence, high performance computing, and biological systems" and "revolutionary omics platforms that enable unprecedented spatial and temporal scales and resolution of physiological and disease mechanisms." Cytoverse addresses both requirements as a single, integrated system. It ingests multi-omic data (single-cell RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, proteomics, metabolomics, spatial transcriptomics) and constructs a learned biological coordinate system, a continuous manifold, where every individual's health state occupies a position. Unlike conventional omics analysis that produces static snapshots, Cytoverse tracks temporal trajectories through this manifold, achieving the "unprecedented temporal scales" the ISO envisions.

The Cytoverse manifold enables what HSF describes as the ability to "definitively diagnose" chronic diseases including diabetes, autoimmune conditions, and neurodegenerative disorders. Current diagnosis relies on binary ICD codes and threshold-based biomarker cutoffs that miss the continuous biological reality. In Cytoverse, a trajectory drifting toward the Alzheimer's disease attractor basin becomes detectable years before clinical symptom onset, transforming diagnosis from a binary event into a continuous monitoring process. This directly satisfies the HSF interest in "paradigm shifting technologies that will change how we approach the diagnosis, treatment, and impact of diseases."

### Secondary Target: Integrated Sensing with On-Device Intelligence

The HSF ISO highlights interest in "integrated sensing and therapy delivery devices for addressing chronic health conditions." The Cytoscope-Cytonome system provides this integration. Cytoscope (The Sensor) performs continuous, multiplexed biomarker measurement using programmable aptamer arrays. Cytonome (The Navigator) runs causal AI inference on-device at less than 5 milliwatts, interpreting Cytoscope signals in real-time against the individual's Cytoverse trajectory. When Cytonome detects trajectory deviation toward a disease attractor, it can trigger therapeutic responses through the same biosensing platform, closing the sense-interpret-intervene loop that HSF envisions.

This integrated system satisfies the HSF criterion for "adaptable, multi-application systems and technologies that are reconfigurable for a wide variety of clinical needs." Because Cytoverse is disease-agnostic, the same Cytoscope hardware and Cytonome firmware can monitor cardiovascular risk, metabolic syndrome, neuroinflammation, or autoimmune flare activity simply by updating the target biomarker panel and the Cytoverse reference trajectory. This reconfigurability at the software level, rather than requiring new hardware per disease category, directly addresses the "multi-application" and "reconfigurable" requirements.

### Stretch Target: Neuropsychiatric Health Monitoring

HSF seeks technologies that expand "the precision, scale, and accessibility of brain circuit mapping technologies that enable causative neuropsychiatric links to mental health disorders leading to definitive diagnosis and reliable therapeutic monitoring." While Cytognosis does not directly perform brain circuit mapping, Cytoverse constructs biological coordinate representations from peripheral biomarkers (circulating proteins, metabolites, cell-free DNA methylation patterns) that correlate with central nervous system states. This peripheral-to-central inference, powered by causal models in Cytonome, enables non-invasive, continuous monitoring of neuropsychiatric trajectories without requiring direct neural recording. The approach complements traditional brain circuit mapping by providing a scalable, accessible, and continuous monitoring layer that traditional neuroimaging cannot achieve at population scale.

## Portfolio Differentiation (Mandatory for MO ISO Submission)

ARPA-H requires proposers to review the full program portfolio and demonstrate that their idea falls outside the scope of existing research. For HSF, the primary overlapping programs are BDF Toolbox and RAPID, plus awareness of PHO-office programs (PROSPR, EVIDENT, OCULAB) that share thematic space.

### vs. BDF Toolbox (MEDIUM overlap, HSF)

BDF Toolbox builds biomedical data fabric infrastructure for integrating and sharing data across institutions. Cytognosis is an analytical platform that generates new insights.

**Use in proposal:** "Cytognosis and the BDF Toolbox operate at different layers of the biomedical data stack. BDF provides infrastructure for integrating and sharing existing datasets across institutions. Cytoverse is an analytical layer that ingests multi-omic data to construct a learned biological coordinate system. Cytoverse could consume BDF-integrated datasets as training inputs, and Cytoscope-generated continuous biomarker data could flow into BDF infrastructure for broader research use."

### vs. RAPID (LOW overlap, HSF)

RAPID uses AI/ML for rare disease diagnostics via phenotype-to-genotype pathways.

**Use in proposal:** "RAPID applies AI to diagnose patients with existing rare diseases through phenotype-driven genomic interpretation. Cytognosis applies AI to detect trajectory deviations in the general population *before any diagnosis*, using multi-omic manifold learning rather than single-gene variant interpretation. RAPID diagnoses the sick; Cytognosis identifies the healthy who are drifting toward illness."

### Cross-Reference: PHO Programs

Although these are PHO-office programs, ARPA-H reviewers assess portfolio overlap across all offices. When submitting to HSF, include awareness of:

- **PROSPR**: Aging therapeutics. Cytognosis is detection, not treatment.
- **EVIDENT**: Behavioral health endpoint validation. Cytognosis is upstream detection.
- **OCULAB**: Single-modality tear sensors. Cytognosis is multimodal computational.
