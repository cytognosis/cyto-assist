# ARPA-H Health Science Futures (HSF) Guidelines

## Mission Overview
The Health Science Futures (HSF) mission office expands what is technically possible by developing approaches that remove scientific and technological limitations stymieing healthcare progress. It focuses on disease-agnostic platforms, breakthrough technologies, and transformative tools.

## Key Interest Areas
1. **Breakthrough Technologies**: Paradigm-shifting tech changing diagnosis/treatment (e.g., maternal/fetal monitoring tech, tissue/organ replacement therapies, high-res brain circuit mapping).
2. **Transformative Tools**: Agile solutions bridging bench-to-bedside (e.g., biohybrid devices, site-selective neuromodulation, synthetic biology logic mechanisms, highly portable/low-radiation physics-based imaging).
3. **Platform Systems**: Adaptable, multi-application systems (e.g., molecular delivery platforms minimizing off-target effects, mammalian/microbial cellular engineering, AI/quantum computing in biological systems).

## Exclusions
**Do NOT propose**:
- Evolutionary or incremental advances.
- Clinical trials for otherwise developed products.
- Center formation, standard education, or physical infrastructure.

## Application Mechanism & Flow
This uses an **Innovative Solutions Opening (ISO)** awarding **Other Transactions (OTs)**.
- **Step 1: Solution Summary**: A short, high-level pitch. Must receive encouraging feedback from ARPA-H before proceeding to Step 2.
- **Step 2: Technical & Management Proposal**: Max 20 pages. Focuses heavily on the technical approach, milestones, and statement of work.

## Evaluation Criteria (Descending Order)
1. **Overall Scientific and Technical Merit**: Is the approach innovative, feasible, and complete? Are risks identified with mitigation plans (Plan B)?
2. **Potential Contribution & Relevance to ARPA-H Mission**: Does it address unmet needs and transform biomedicine? What is the commercialization strategy?
3. **Proposer's Capabilities & Related Experience**: Does the team have the track record to pull off this high-risk endeavor?
4. **Cost/Price/Budget Assessment**: Risk and budget should align. Do not submit artificially low budgets or low-risk ideas to appear more appealing.

## Strategic Positioning
- Similar to DARPA, focus on "ARPA-H Hard" problems. Prove that the science/engineering limits are being fundamentally pushed.
- Use milestones effectively. Given the OT mechanism, funding is highly flexible but dependent on objective success at defined technical junctures.
