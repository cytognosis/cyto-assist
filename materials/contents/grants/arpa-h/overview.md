# ARPA-H Proposals

ARPA-H does **not** issue grants. Funding is via **contracts, cooperative agreements, or other transactions (OTs)**.

## Organization

ARPA-H has **4 Mission Offices**, each with its own ISO:

| Office | Focus | ISO Solicitation |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| **PHO** (Proactive Health Office) | Prevention, early detection, healthspan | ARPA-H-SOL-24-106 |
| **HSF** (Health Science Futures) | Disease-agnostic platforms, breakthrough tech | ARPA-H-SOL-24-104 |
| **SSO** (Scalable Solutions Office) | Scaling proven health solutions | ARPA-H-SOL-24-105 |
| **RSO** (Resilient Systems Office) | Health system resilience | ARPA-H-SOL-24-107 |

Cytognosis targets **PHO** (proactive health, early detection) and **HSF** (platform technology).

## Funding Categories

[ARPA-H funding](https://arpa-h.gov/explore-funding/open-funding-opportunities) falls into:
- **Programs** — Targeted programs with specific technical goals (e.g., PROSPR, EVIDENT)
- **Initiatives & Sprints** — Shorter-term, focused efforts
- **Small Business Awards** — SBIR/STTR equivalents
- **Mission Office ISOs** — Broad solicitations accepting any innovative idea within the office scope

We are applying via **Mission Office ISOs** (PHO and HSF).

> [!CAUTION]
> ## Mandatory Portfolio Overlap Check
> 
> Before submitting to **any** ARPA-H Mission Office ISO, you **must** verify that the proposed idea does NOT overlap with the scope of existing ARPA-H programs or initiatives.
> 
> From ARPA-H's website:
> > *"Familiarize yourself with the ARPA-H research portfolio: Review all research in the ARPA-H Program Portfolio and Initiatives & Sprints to ensure your idea falls outside the scope of existing research. A submission to a Mission Office ISO may be rejected if it's within the scope of an existing program or initiative."*
> 
> **Action required:**
> 1. Review the full portfolio at [arpa-h.gov/programs](https://arpa-h.gov/programs)
> 2. Check the detailed program profiles in `arpa-h-portfolio.md`
> 3. Explicitly differentiate the proposal from any related programs (especially **PROSPR** and **EVIDENT** for Cytognosis)
> 4. Document in the proposal how the approach is distinct

## PHO ISO (Proactive Health Office)

**Solicitation:** ARPA-H-SOL-24-106 | **Deadline:** Rolling to March 2029

**Focus areas aligned with Cytognosis:**
- Novel prevention and early detection methods
- Scalable sensing technologies and low-cost diagnostics
- Population-level wellness and proactive health
- System-level innovations for healthcare delivery

### Step 1: Solution Summary (required first)

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Title** | Concise, descriptive (< 100 chars) |
| **Problem** | Specific healthcare gap (1-2 paragraphs) |
| **Proposed Solution** | Technical approach, novelty, disruptive potential |
| **Cytognosis Differentiator** | Why our platform uniquely solves this |
| **Impact** | Quantitative outcomes, population reach, timeline |
| **Team** | Key personnel qualifications |

**Submit via:** ARPA-H Solution Submission Portal

### Step 2: Full Proposal (by invitation after favorable feedback)

- 45 calendar days to submit after encouragement
- Must use ARPA-H templates
- Sections: Technical, Management, Cost, Policy
- Cost proposal workbook with detailed financials
