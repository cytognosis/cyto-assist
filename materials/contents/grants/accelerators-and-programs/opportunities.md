## 1. QB3 Early-Stage Mentoring

**URL**: [qb3.org/early-stage-mentoring](https://qb3.org/early-stage-mentoring/)
**Contact**: Kaspar Mossman, PhD (Managing Director), <kmossman@berkeley.edu>, 415-699-2562

### Program

- Free mentoring (no fee)
- Match with industry mentors advising on: technology-market fit, academic/commercial separation, IP strategy, fundraising, regulatory path (IND/510k)
- Community access to Bakar Bio Labs (UC Berkeley) events, speakers, networking
- **$100K proof-of-concept funding**: Grant to UC academic lab OR $100K in-kind rent at Bakar Bio Labs via SAFE note (contingent on incubator acceptance)
- Exposure to BEVC (affiliated VC firm)

### Costs/Terms

- No fee for program
- After 3-month trial, if productive: mentor receives equity as consultant
  - Pre-incorporation: none
  - Recently incorporated: 1% vesting over months 4-6
  - Seed funded: 0.5%
  - Series A funded: 0.25%

### Eligibility

- Pre-incorporation or recently incorporated biotech with **wet-lab component**
- More than just a concept; initial data required
- California-based or intending to relocate
- No university affiliation required

### 2026 Deadlines

- ~~February 13~~ (passed)
- **May 29, 2026**
- **October 9, 2026**

### Previous Feedback (Kaspar Mossman)

Key objections from previous application:

1. "Diagnostics is a hard field to succeed in for a startup"
2. "I am not sure what you intend to diagnose"
3. "There aren't any broadly accepted biomarkers for most neurodegenerative conditions"
4. "Even for those that do have biomarkers, there are no therapies, or only very limited ones"
5. "If you DO diagnose something, whether you can do anything about it"
6. "For something that you plan to diagnose many years ahead of time, how does one design a clinical trial for that?"
7. Business model unaddressed

### Reapplication Strategy

If reapplying, must address each objection:

1. Frame as **platform technology**, not single-indication diagnostic
2. Specify concrete first indications (cancer, cardiovascular) with existing biomarkers AND therapies
3. Position pre-symptomatic detection as **risk stratification** for existing treatments, not standalone diagnosis
4. Address clinical trial design: longitudinal cohort with standard-of-care endpoint enrichment
5. Business model: B2B health system integration, not DTC

## 2. Brains Accelerator (Speculative Technologies)

**URL**: [spec.tech/brains](https://spec.tech/brains)
**Contact**: <brains@spec.tech>
**Organization**: Speculative Technologies (nonprofit), supported by Emergent Ventures

### Program Structure

- **15-week, part-time accelerator** (10 hrs/week commitment)
- In-person kickoff (3 days) + virtual weekly sessions + showcase
- 1:1 mentoring from experienced individuals (including DARPA alumni)
- No direct grants, but network access to funders, partners, and peers
- Goal: help participants refine ideas to launch FROs or obtain roles at ARPA-like organizations

### Application (2025/2026 Cohort)

- Applications opened: October 1, 2025
- Deadline: November 18, 2025
- Admission decisions: January 12, 2026
- Program dates: Late February to June 2026
- **6 short answer questions** (loosely based on Heilmeier Catechism)

### Eligibility

- Deep technical background (PhD or equivalent research)
- Several years of work experience
- Ambitious ideas that don't fit existing institutional structures
- Experience within AND outside academia
- Strong bias towards action, comfort with uncertainty
- Interest in pursuing ideas through new roles or non-VC organizations
- No US citizenship or location requirements

### Heilmeier Catechism Questions (Template)

1. What are you trying to do? Articulate your objectives using absolutely no jargon.
2. How is it done today, and what are the limits of current practice?
3. What is new in your approach and why do you think it will be successful?
4. Who cares? If you succeed, what difference will it make?
5. What are the risks?
6. How much will it cost? How long will it take?

### Previous Application

- Applied Winter 2025, rejected December 19, 2025
- Existing application: `materials/drafts/applications/brains.md`
- Rejection was non-specific ("limited slots")

### Reapplication Strategy

- Next cohort: likely applications ~October 2026
- Strengthen: concrete milestones with timeline, clearer FRO-to-operation plan
- Emphasize: Cytognosis already operates as FRO (not aspiring to become one)

**URL**: [drkfoundation.org/apply](https://www.drkfoundation.org/apply/)
**Application form**: [tfaforms.com/4887099](https://www.tfaforms.com/4887099)

#### Funding

- **$300,000 over 3 years** (unrestricted grants for nonprofits, investment capital for for-profits)

- Rolling applications, no fixed deadline

#### Eligibility

- Early-stage, post-pilot, pre-scale (2-5 years old)
- NOT idea-stage, pre-pilot, or post-Series A
- 501(c)(3) nonprofits, B Corps, C Corps, fiscally sponsored projects
- Must demonstrate potential to **impact 10,000+ lives within 5 years**
- Scalable, data-driven approach
- Financially sustainable or credible income plan
- Geographic focus: Africa, Europe, India, **United States**
- Founders fully committed or planning to go full-time

#### Application Questions

1. **Problem and Solution**:
   - Mission and objectives
   - Social problem addressed
   - Solution provided
2. **Impact**:
   - How you solve the problem better than existing efforts
   - How you serve underserved populations (pilot benefits)
   - What makes your approach unique
   - Tangible results to date and measurement plan
3. **Scale**:
   - How you plan to scale over time
   - Main sources of earned income for sustainability
4. **Leader**:
   - Standard resume/CV for key leadership (NO text bios)

#### Cytognosis Fit

**STRONG**: Nonprofit 501(c)(3) addressing health equity, US-based, has pilot-stage platform (Cytoverse), can demonstrate potential 10K+ lives impacted. Frame as: precision health platform that democratizes disease detection for underserved populations who lack access to specialized diagnostics.
