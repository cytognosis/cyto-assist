## Organization Overview

**Wellcome Leap** is a nonprofit "global ARPA for health" funded by the Wellcome Trust (UK). Founded in 2020. Led by former DARPA director Regina Dugan. Builds bold, unconventional programs funded at scale ($45-140M each), designed to deliver breakthroughs in 3-5 years.

**Key numbers**: 14 programs executed in 5 years, across 30 countries, with 170+ network institutions on 6 continents representing 1,500,000+ researchers.

**Operating principles** (from "Changing the Business of Breakthroughs"):

1. **Ambitious, testable goals**: Every program has a measurable "what if" question
2. **Coordinated multidisciplinary networks**: Not individual labs, orchestrated performer teams
3. **PM-led**: Program Director acts as conductor, makes decisions, avoids groupthink
4. **Urgency via deadlines**: 3-5 year timelines; if 2 weeks pass without progress, 1% of time is lost
5. **Contracts not grants**: Suppresses "grantsmanship," elevates outcomes, enables risk-taking (fund year 1, decide on years 2-3 based on results)
6. **Pasteur's Quadrant**: Use-inspired fundamental research, not pure basic nor pure applied

> "The proposal is not the work; the work is the work."

## Programs with Highest Cytognosis Relevance

### Dynamic Resilience ($60M, Active)

**What if our health span could match our life span?**

- **Scope**: Develop novel multimodal biomarkers of resilience that predict and track transition from health to frailty. Goal: reduce progression to frailty by 25%.
- **Focus**: 80% of adults 65+ have at least one chronic condition; 30% are "pre-frail." Seeks to identify biochemical, physiological, and digital markers that define resilience vs. decline.
- **PD**: Lynne Cox
- **Why relevant**: Shares the healthspan focus with Cytognosis, but Dynamic Resilience targets aging-specific resilience biomarkers and interventions, while Cytognosis builds disease-agnostic cellular intelligence infrastructure. Cytoscope continuous sensing could feed biomarker data into Dynamic Resilience trajectories. Cytoverse could provide the biological coordinate system that gives Dynamic Resilience's biomarkers meaning in context.

### Delta Tissue ($55M, Completed)

**What if we could build a "tissue time machine" to predict future disease progression with the fidelity needed to intervene in the present?**

- **Scope**: Build multiscale, multimodal platforms for profiling tissue states and predicting transitions between states. Test cases: Crohn's disease, glioblastoma.
- **PD**: Jason Swedlow (co-founder of Open Microscopy Environment)
- **Focus**: Currently, treatment decisions rely on population-level data ("30% response rate, but we don't know which 30%"). Delta Tissue builds individually targeted prediction.
- **Why relevant**: Conceptually aligned with Cytoverse, but at the tissue level rather than the cellular/molecular level. Cytoverse operates on multi-omic single-cell data to construct health states; Delta Tissue operates on tissue imaging to predict disease progression. These are complementary scales of the same problem.

## Health Breakthrough Network (HBNet)

### Overview

Largest, most rapidly activatable health research network in the world. 170+ member institutions across 6 continents. Established January 2021 with 21 charter institutions.

### How to Join

Email **<HBNet@wellcomeleap.org>**. Open to universities, research institutions, nonprofits, and commercial entities. Membership requires signing either MARFA or CORFA.

### MARFA / CORFA Agreements

**MARFA** = Master Academic Research Funding Agreement (for academic institutions)
**CORFA** = Commercial Research Funding Agreement (for companies, nonprofits)

These are **pre-negotiated master agreements** that handle all terms upfront. Once signed, only a Statement of Work (SOW) and cost need to be negotiated per project, enabling project starts in **days** instead of months.

#### IP Ownership

| Item | Terms |
| ---- | ----- |
| **IP ownership** | IP stays with the Performer's organization |
| **Wellcome Leap license** | Non-exclusive license for R&D purposes only; sublicensable to nonprofits only for continued development |
| **Joint inventions** | Wellcome Leap assigns its interest to the Performer; retains non-exclusive R&D license |
| **Background IP** | Performer must identify background IP needed for the research; non-exclusive R&D license required if able |
| **Override clause** | If Performer can show the license would hinder Program goals (scaling, distribution), Wellcome Leap will reconsider |

#### Publication

| Item | Terms |
| ---- | ----- |
| **Publication policy** | Open access prioritized |
| **Review period** | 30 days for Wellcome Leap to review for confidential info or patentable subject matter |
| **Patent delay** | Up to 30 additional days if patentable subject matter identified |

#### Confidentiality

- Information of "obviously confidential nature" is protected with or without explicit marking
- No strict marking requirement (to avoid limiting collaborative exchange or creating "gotchas")
- Performers can mark their own confidential information at their discretion

#### Commercialization

| Item | Terms |
| ---- | ----- |
| **Expectation** | Wellcome Leap expects commercialization of results |
| **Step-in rights** | If Performer not making appropriate commercialization efforts: Wellcome Leap requests meeting → remedial plan → 6-month remediation period → step-in as extraordinary remedy |
| **Step-in nature** | "Extraordinary remedy" that Wellcome Leap states it would not exercise under any but "most unusual circumstance" |
| **Financial participation** | Performer may still participate financially post-step-in |

#### Termination

| Trigger | Terms |
| ------- | ----- |
| MARFA/CORFA termination | Either party for uncured breach; Wellcome Leap with 30-day notice (extraordinary); Performer if no pending SOWs |
| SOW termination | Wellcome Leap for convenience (extraordinary); either party if R&D no longer feasible (30-day notice) |
| Performer SOW restriction | Performer cannot terminate an active SOW for convenience (to protect integrated program integrity) |

#### Eligible Costs

Full direct and government-certified indirect costs appropriate to the research execution.

## How Programs and Proposals Work

### Program Creation (Top-Down)

Wellcome Leap does **NOT** have an open solicitation for new programs (unlike ARPA-H's Mission Office ISOs). The pipeline:

1. **Wellcome Leap leadership identifies** a breakthrough opportunity fitting Pasteur's Quadrant
2. **A Program Director is recruited/hired** specifically to lead that program (PDs are temporary; they come to "run a project that can change the world")
3. **The PD designs** the program structure, thrust areas, "what-if" question, performer requirements, and milestones
4. **A Program Announcement** is published with specific solicitation criteria

### Proposing Research (Bottom-Up)

Researchers engage by responding to **active program solicitations**:

1. **Phase 1: Abstract** submission via online portal (with draft budget). Templates and guidelines provided per program. Not necessary to be an HBNet member to apply.
2. **Phase 2: Full Proposal** (up to 25 pages, 12pt font, 0.5" margins, PDF). Structured peer review by PD + internal/external experts.
3. **Selection**: If selected, PI's organization must sign MARFA/CORFA (can be signed in advance via HBNet).
4. **Execution**: SOW + cost negotiation → work begins (often in days for HBNet members).

**Eligibility**: Globally open. Universities, research institutions, small-to-large companies (including venture-backed), government, nonprofits. Individual or small team applications encouraged.

**Confidentiality**: Ideas treated as confidential during review. May use third-party AI for processing.

**Contact**: Program-specific emails (e.g., <care@wellcomeleap.org>, <save@wellcomeleap.org>). PDs are not available to meet applicants during abstract/proposal phases.

### Engagement Strategy for Cytognosis

1. **Join HBNet now**: Email <HBNet@wellcomeleap.org> to start MARFA negotiation. Being in the network enables rapid activation when a relevant program launches.
2. **Monitor for new programs**: Dynamic Resilience is active and most aligned. Future programs in continuous health monitoring, AI-driven diagnostics, or biosensing would be direct targets.
3. **Build relationships**: Attend Wellcome Leap events (Aspen Ideas Health, Forbes Power Women's Summit). The PD recruitment channel is key; if Shahin's profile fits a program concept, a PD conversation could lead to program co-design.
4. **No unsolicited proposals**: There is no mechanism to propose a new program. Influence happens through the PD recruitment pipeline and through performing on existing programs.

## Comparison: Wellcome Leap vs. ARPA-H

| Dimension | Wellcome Leap | ARPA-H |
| --------- | ------------- | ------ |
| **Funder** | Wellcome Trust (UK philanthropy) | US federal government |
| **Legal entity** | Nonprofit (US-based) | Federal agency (HHS) |
| **Program creation** | Top-down: PD recruited, designs program | Top-down (programs) + bottom-up (MO ISOs) |
| **Open solicitation** | Only for active programs | Programs + always-open MO ISOs |
| **Program size** | $45-140M each | $10-100M+ each |
| **Timeline** | 3-5 years | 3-5 years |
| **IP model** | IP stays with performer; non-exclusive R&D license | Government Purpose Rights; prefers open-source |
| **Award mechanism** | Contracts (via MARFA/CORFA + SOW) | Other Transactions (OTs), grants, contracts |
| **Global scope** | Yes (30 countries, 6 continents) | Primarily US (some international) |
| **Network** | HBNet (170+ institutions) | No equivalent membership model |
| **Open science** | Open access publication prioritized | "Strongly encourages" open-source IP |
| **Commercialization** | Step-in rights as extraordinary remedy | Government license + transition plan required |
