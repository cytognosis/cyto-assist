## 1. UK ARIA (Advanced Research + Invention Agency)

**Model**: DARPA-style, Programme Director-led

ARIA empowers scientists and engineers with resources and freedom to pursue breakthroughs "at the edge of the possible." Programme Directors define multi-million-pound research programs and distribute funding. Many projects will fail, but successes generate massive social and economic returns.

### Massively Scalable Neurotechnologies (Top Priority)

**Deadline**: Concept papers by **March 17, 2026** (TA1 Phase 1 only; TA2/TA3 launching Summer 2026)

Three technical areas:

1. **Delivery + Performance**: Responsive neural interfaces reaching deep brain targets without transcranial surgery
2. **Prototyping + Translation**: Infrastructure for rapid iteration, regulatory navigation, manufacturing, clinical readiness
3. **Adoption**: Public understanding, lived experience storytelling

Key quote: "Some of the most radical solutions may come from Creators who do not traditionally consider themselves neurotechnologists. Whether you are a synthetic biologist developing engineered cells or a biotechnologist developing new vectors to access the brain, we want to hear from you."

**Cytognosis angle**: Cytoscope biosensing platform as non-traditional approach to neural interfaces. Cytoverse AI coordinate system for neural state mapping. On-device Cytonome for real-time neural signal interpretation.

### Non-EU Eligibility

- **Associated Countries**: UK is NOT currently a Horizon Europe associated country (post-Brexit)
- **Third-country participation**: Non-EU entities can participate but typically NOT receive funding
- **Exception**: If UK associates to Horizon Europe (negotiations ongoing), UK entities become eligible
- **Workaround**: Partner with EU-based institution as lead; Cytognosis as subcontractor or associated partner
- **Relevance for UK office**: If Cytognosis opens UK office, monitor UK-Horizon Europe association status

### Key Program Types

| Type | Description | Typical Budget |
| --- | --- | --- |
| RIA (Research and Innovation Action) | Collaborative research, TRL 1-5 | €2-5M per project |
| IA (Innovation Action) | Closer to market, TRL 5-8 | €2-10M per project |
| CSA (Coordination and Support Action) | Networking, coordination | €1-3M |

### Application Process

- Applications via **Funding & Tenders Portal** (ec.europa.eu/funding-tenders)
- Consortia required (typically 3+ partners from 3+ EU/associated countries)
- Two-stage evaluation: administrative eligibility, then scientific excellence/impact/implementation
- Proposal structure: Part A (admin forms), Part B (technical proposal, typically 45-70 pages)

## 3. NIH Grant Mechanisms (Expanded)

### Mechanism Comparison Table

| Mechanism | Name | Duration | Budget | Prelim Data | Cytognosis Fit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **R01** | Research Project Grant | Up to 5 years | No cap (>$500K/yr needs prior approval) | Expected | **HIGH** (established research) |
| **R21** | Exploratory/Developmental | 2 years | $275K total ($200K/yr max) | Not required | **HIGH** (early-stage, high-risk) |
| **P30** | Center Core Grant | 5 years (renewable) | Variable | Required | Medium (shared resources) |
| **U54** | Specialized Center Cooperative | Variable | $2-10M+ | Required | **HIGH** (Bridge2AI model) |
| **U01** | Cooperative Agreement | 3-5 years | Variable | Variable | Medium |
| **R43/R44** | SBIR (Small Business) | Phase I: 6mo, Phase II: 2yr | Phase I: $275K, Phase II: $1.75M | Variable | NOT eligible (nonprofit) |
| **R41/R42** | STTR (Small Business Tech Transfer) | Same as SBIR | Same as SBIR | Variable | Eligible as research partner only |

### R01 Details

- **"Gold standard"** of NIH grants
- Supports well-defined, discrete projects based on investigator's specific interests
- Typical: $250K-$500K direct costs per year for 3-5 years
- Prior approval needed if >$500K direct costs in any single year
- Preliminary data expected (strongly recommended)
- **2026 New Application Deadlines**: February 5, June 5, October 5

### R21 Details

- **Exploratory/developmental** research
- High-risk, high-reward studies for novel techniques, methods, models
- **No preliminary data required** (but recommended)
- Max $275K direct costs total over 2-year period ($200K max per year)
- Research strategy: max 6 pages
- Good stepping stone to generate data for R01
- **2026 New Application Deadlines**: February 16, June 16, October 16

### P30 Center Core Grants

- Support shared resources and facilities for multiple investigators
- Multidisciplinary approach, collaborative research
- Enhance productivity beyond individual projects
- **Cytognosis relevance**: Could contribute as a core facility or shared resource in a P30 center focused on computational biology or precision medicine

### U54 Specialized Centers

- Broad spectrum: basic to clinical science
- Developed in response to specific programmatic needs
- Often serve as regional/national resources
- **Bridge2AI**: NIH Common Fund used U54 for their AI in biomedical research program
- **Cytognosis relevance**: Strong fit as a specialized center for cellular intelligence/disease biology mapping, especially if modeling after Bridge2AI
