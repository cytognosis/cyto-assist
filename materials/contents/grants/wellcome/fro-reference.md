# Wellcome Leap & FRO Strategy

## Wellcome Leap Programs

**Model:** DARPA-style for global health | **Scale:** $50M per program, 5-10 year moonshots

### Proposal Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Moonshot Question** | Frame as "What if..." (matches their format) |
| **Scientific Rationale** | Why now? What has changed? |
| **Technical Approach** | Disruptive, not incremental |
| **Milestones** | Seemingly impossible results, measurable |
| **Team & Network** | Multi-institution, global collaboration |
| **Budget** | Subcontract model, typically $2-10M per performer |

### Cytognosis Pitch Angle

> "What if we could detect disease 10 years before the first symptom, in every person, at the cost of a blood pressure cuff?"

Align with active programs: Dynamic Resilience (healthspan = lifespan), Delta Tissue (predict future disease progression), VISIBLE FORM (precision diagnostics).

## Convergent Research Focused Research Organizations (FROs)

**Model:** Purpose-built nonprofits for scientific infrastructure | **Duration:** 3-7 years

### FRO Abstract Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Executive Summary** | 1-page overview |
| **The Gap** | What cannot be done in academia, startups, or national labs? |
| **Our Solution** | Systematic engineering approach to fill the gap |
| **Why a FRO is Essential** | Why a new nonprofit org is needed (see justification below) |
| **Team & Timeline** | Engineers + scientists (not just trainees), industry wages |
| **Impact & Open Science Commitment** | Outputs must be public goods |
| **Funding Request & Sustainability** | Typically $20-50M over 3-5 years |
| **The Convergent Opportunity** | Why partner with Convergent specifically |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid (from Convergent)

1. "Just do what my lab does, but with more money" — must need new org structure
2. "Hire 20 great researchers for blue-sky projects" — must be coordinated engineering effort
3. "Do my startup idea with nonprofit funding" — if viable as startup, do that instead
4. "Tackle the biggest intellectual challenge" — FROs excel at unglamorous tool-building

## Why a FRO: Cytognosis Structural Justification

*Source: Cytognosis [Why FRO](file:///home/mohammadi/repos/cytognosis/agents/Why_FRO.docx) — "Industrializing the Discovery of Disease Biology"*

### The Structural Gap

The pharmaceutical innovation ecosystem is **bifurcated**:
- **Academia**: Excels at foundational discovery, but lacks infrastructure for industrial-scale validation
- **For-Profit Industry**: Excels at compound optimization, but economically constrained to low-risk, patentable assets with short return horizons

This creates a **"Valley of Death"** where valid biological insights languish for decades before translation.

### Three Market Failures

#### 1. The 32-Year Timeline Mismatch

NIH funding contributed to 354 of 356 drugs (99.4%) approved 2010-2019, totaling $187B. But maturation takes an average of **32 years** from discovery to FDA approval — fundamentally incompatible with 10-year fund lifecycles.

**Sevabertinib case study**: Exon 20 insertions identified at Broad Institute in 2005. Drug approved November 2025. The 8-year "dormant period" (2005-2013) existed because academia lacked screening infrastructure and industry deemed the biology too risky. Success required an ad-hoc alliance (Broad + Bayer, 2013). **Cytognosis institutionalizes this synergy** — housing both deep biological exploration and high-throughput screening, compressing dormant years into active engineering.

#### 2. The "Commons" Problem: Unpatentable Biology

US patents protect **compounds, not biological mechanisms**. Target Identification accounts for only 3-7% of R&D costs, yet governs >90% of downstream attrition risk. Companies invest in patentable molecules, not in understanding disease biology — creating a public goods problem.

#### 3. The Prevention Paradox and ICD Code Limitations

- Preventative therapies require larger populations + longer trials — economically prohibitive for for-profit
- ICD codes are **symptom-centric**, creating artificial "disease silos" that blind researchers to cross-disorder mechanisms
- Cytognosis replaces binary sick/healthy with **continuous spectrum mapping** along biological axes

### Cytognosis as the Third Pillar

Cytognosis fits **neither** the academic model (which rewards publications > standardization) **nor** the for-profit model (which requires patent protection + rapid ROI). It is the **necessary third pillar**:

1. **De-risk the public good**: Centralized hub for validating disease targets (like AddGene for genetic material)
2. **Continuous spectrum mapping**: Track individuals along biological trajectories, not arbitrary symptom thresholds
3. **Industrialize validation**: Replicate the "Sevabertinib model" systematically, eliminating years of lag

### When to Use This Justification

Use in proposals for:
- **Convergent Research**: "Why a FRO is Essential" section — core requirement
- **NSF Tech Labs**: "Why not a traditional academic lab or startup?" question
- **Wellcome Leap**: Scientific Rationale — "Why now?" framing
- **Foresight / EA / Astera**: Mission and structural justification
