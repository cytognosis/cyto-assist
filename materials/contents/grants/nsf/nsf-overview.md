## NSF Organization: Directorate for Technology, Innovation and Partnerships (TIP)

**NSF TIP** is the newest NSF directorate, created under the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022. Led by **Erwin Gianchandani** (Assistant Director). NSF TIP hosts the programs most relevant to Cytognosis.

### Three Strategic Pillars

1. **Accelerate critical and emerging technology**: Fund breakthrough research translations in quantum, AI, biotechnology, semiconductors, critical materials
2. **Expand the geography of American innovation**: Regional hubs, manufacturing, domestic capacity
3. **Build a competition-ready workforce**: Training in emerging technologies, accessible pathways to future jobs

## NSF Tech Labs ⭐ (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

### Overview

The most important NSF opportunity for Cytognosis. Purpose-built for FRO-like organizations.

**What it is**: A bold, flexible, outcomes-driven initiative to fund full-time research, development, and innovation (RDI) teams focused on overcoming persistent barriers to commercialization of emerging technologies. These teams operate with operational autonomy, milestone-based funding, and the ability to engage across academia, industry, national labs, and nonprofits.

**Funding**: $10-50M per team per year, multi-year (4+ years)

**Mechanism**: Other Transaction (OT) contracts (not grants), enabling greater flexibility, operational autonomy, and reduced administrative burden

**Topics**: Quantum technology, artificial intelligence, critical materials, semiconductor manufacturing, **biotechnology**

**Timeline**:

- RFI closed: January 20, 2026
- Webinars held: Dec 17, 2025 and Jan 14, 2026
- **Solicitation expected: Spring 2026**
- Selections: First half 2026

### Why Cytognosis Is a Perfect Fit

Tech Labs was designed for organizations exactly like Cytognosis:

| Tech Labs Requirement | Cytognosis Alignment |
| --------------------- | -------------------- |
| "Independent research organizations" | 501(c)(3) nonprofit FRO, operationally independent |
| "Overcoming persistent barriers to commercialization" | Platform technology (Cytoverse/Cytoscope) bridging research-to-deployment gap |
| "Full-time, dedicated teams" | FRO model: full-time researchers, not adjunct PI labs |
| "Operational autonomy, milestone-based funding" | Mission-driven, milestone-ready, not grant-dependent |
| "Emerging technologies with platform potential" | AI health coordinate system = platform technology (like PCR, internet examples in RFI) |
| "Use-inspired research (Pasteur's Quadrant)" | Fundamental AI/biology research with direct health application |
| "FROs, incubator teams, independent teams" | Cytognosis is literally a focused research organization |
| "Biotechnology" as named topic area | Core domain |
| "De-risk technologies not ready for private investment" | Pre-revenue platform R&D, too early for VC but too applied for NIH |
| "Nonprofit and philanthropy partnerships" | Wellcome Trust alignment, open-source commitment |

### Program Phases

| Phase | Duration | Description | Funding |
| ----- | -------- | ----------- | ------- |
| Selection | 90 days | 5-8 page abstract, oral pitch | N/A |
| Phase 0 | 9 months | Develop concepts, recruit team, refine milestones, demonstrate early progress. IP + Research Security plans required. | OT contract |
| Phase 1 | 24 months | Scale to 10-50 members, execute on milestones | $10-50M/yr |
| Phase 2 | 24+ months | Scale solutions, transition to industry, spin off | $10-50M/yr |

### Selection Criteria (Phase 0)

1. **Team Qualifications and Structure**
   - Technical, leadership, and creative expertise
   - Legal and organizational structure appropriate for autonomous work
2. **Mission Clarity and Outcomes**
   - Addresses challenge/gap/bottleneck within specified topic
   - Alignment with Tech Labs strategic objectives
   - Well-defined, appropriately ambitious activities and outcomes

### Strategic Objectives (address majority, not all)

1. **Build Sector-Defining Platforms**: Novel platform technologies as foundation for future innovation (examples: VLSI, internet, PCR, LEDs)
2. **Generate Outcomes Beyond Papers**: Technology adoption, TRL advancement, startup ecosystem emergence, investment interest
3. **De-risk Emerging Technologies**: Mature tech not yet ready for private investment
4. **Identify and Address Market Needs**: Industry/philanthropy partnerships for adoption
5. **Lead Novel Partnerships**: Co-develop solutions with industry, philanthropy, nonprofits

### Key Design Features

- **Bet on teams, not projects**: Fund full-time dedicated teams, not individual project grants
- **Lightweight application**: 5-8 page abstract (vs. typical 15+ page NSF proposals)
- **OT contracts, not grants**: Milestone-based, no traditional NSF grant overhead
- **Team may evolve**: Personnel and focus can change as milestones are achieved
- **Independent entity required by Phase 1**: Must operate as own entity with organizational autonomy
- **Complementary funding welcomed**: Industry and philanthropic co-funding encouraged

### Eligible Organizations

Broad range:

- Independent pre-formed teams
- Teams from academia or industry ready to scale beyond institutional structure
- Teams in incubator programs
- Recently formed leadership teams planning to scale
- **Focused research organizations (FROs)** with or without current external support

### Tech Labs RFI Questions (for reference)

The RFI asked 6 core questions. Cytognosis should prepare answers for all, especially:

- Q1: Organization description (501(c)(3) FRO, computational biology platform)
- Q3a: Vision for advancing state of practice (Cytoverse, Cytoscope, Cytonome)
- Q3a-iv: Nonprofit/philanthropic engagement (Wellcome Trust alignment, HBNet)
- Q5: Synergy with industry/philanthropy (open-source, health equity, commercial biosensing partners)
- Q6: Translational problem solvable in 3-7 years (pre-symptomatic disease detection via continuous cellular intelligence)

## NSF Tech Accelerators (Companion Initiative)

**Status**: Announced alongside Tech Labs, details to be released shortly after Tech Labs solicitation

**Differences from Tech Labs**:

- Wider variety of entry points
- Focus on de-risking and accelerating technology translation into market/society
- Specific key technology areas critical to national priorities
- Higher volume of translation efforts across entire technology disciplines
- Tech Labs = small number of big bets; Tech Accelerators = larger number of entry points

**For Cytognosis**: Monitor as secondary option. If Tech Labs is the "DARPA program manager" equivalent, Tech Accelerators is the "broader performer ecosystem" equivalent.

## SBIR/STTR (America's Seed Fund)

### Eligibility

| Criterion | SBIR | STTR |
| --------- | ---- | ---- |
| Lead applicant | For-profit small business ONLY | For-profit small business ONLY |
| Nonprofit as lead | **NOT ELIGIBLE** | **NOT ELIGIBLE** |
| Nonprofit as partner | Not required | **YES**: research institution partner required (30% work allocation) |
| Size | <500 employees | <500 employees |
| Ownership | US-owned/operated, 51%+ US citizens | US-owned/operated, 51%+ US citizens |

**Bottom line**: Cytognosis as a 501(c)(3) cannot apply directly to SBIR/STTR. Could partner as the research institution on an STTR with a for-profit spinout or collaborator. IP agreement required between small business and research institution.

### Funding Amounts

| Phase | SBIR | STTR |
| ----- | ---- | ---- |
| Phase I | Up to $256K / 6 months | Up to $256K / 12 months |
| Phase II | Up to $1M / 24 months | Up to $1M / 24 months |

### Relevant SBIR/STTR Topics (if partnering)

NSF SBIR/STTR has topics in:

- Biotech, AI/ML, Advanced Manufacturing, Digital Health
- Could be relevant if Cytognosis creates a for-profit spinout for Cytoscope hardware

## Other Relevant NSF Programs

### Accelerating Research Translation (ART)

- **Funding**: ~$178M across 5 tracks in 2026
- **Awards**: Up to $3M over ~3 years
- **Deadline**: March 12, 2026 (NSF 25-548)
- **Focus**: Building institutional capacity for research commercialization
- **Cytognosis fit**: Medium. More about building translation infrastructure within existing institutions than creating new ones.

### NSF Engines (Regional Innovation Engines)

- **Funding**: $160M+ per engine over 10 years
- **Focus**: Regional innovation ecosystems, place-based
- **Status**: 15 finalists announced in September 2025 for second competition
- **Cytognosis fit**: Medium. Could join as a partner in an existing Engine focused on biotech/health, particularly in the Bay Area. Not a direct lead opportunity.

### Pathways to Enable Open-Source Ecosystems (PESOSE)

- **Focus**: Growing open-source projects into sustainable ecosystems
- **Cytognosis fit**: Medium. Could fund Cytoverse open-source platform development, but scope is software ecosystem management, not scientific research.

### Proto-Open Knowledge Network (Proto-OKN)

- **Focus**: Building open knowledge graph infrastructure
- **Cytognosis fit**: Low-Medium. Cytoverse as a health knowledge graph could align, but scope is general infrastructure.

### PCL Test Bed (Cloud Laboratories)

- **Focus**: Network of programmable cloud laboratories
- **Cytognosis fit**: Medium. Remote/cloud laboratory infrastructure for wet lab experiments at scale.

### Convergence Accelerator (Legacy, now integrated)

- **Focus**: Multi-disciplinary convergence research with phase-based funding
- **Status**: Now integrated into TIP's broader portfolio
- **Cytognosis fit**: Was relevant in prior rounds; future tracks TBD.
