## ARTICLE I: MISSION AND STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK

### Section 1.1: Charitable Purpose

Cytognosis Foundation, Inc. (the "Foundation") is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, as stated in its Certificate of Incorporation.

**Mission:** Revolutionize global healthcare from reactive treatment to proactive prevention through pioneering AI-native technologies that detect and prevent disease before symptoms emerge, with particular emphasis on reducing health disparities and ensuring equitable access to life-saving technologies for underserved populations worldwide.

**No Private Inurement:** No part of the net earnings of the Foundation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Foundation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth herein.

### Section 1.2: Strategic Development Framework

The Foundation shall pursue its mission through adaptive phases that evolve based on technological progress, market conditions, and global health needs:

**PHASE I: RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT**

- Build foundational AI-native health technologies  
- Establish strategic partnerships and collaborations  
- Develop an intellectual property portfolio  
- Secure initial funding and resources

**PHASE II: VALIDATION & COMMERCIALIZATION**

- Clinical and real-world validation of technologies  
- Development of sustainable business models  
- Expansion of impact through strategic deployment  
- Building organizational capacity and infrastructure

**PHASE III: GLOBAL ACCESS & HEALTH EQUITY**

- Worldwide technology deployment and accessibility  
- Philanthropic capital mobilization for subsidized access  
- Policy influence and regulatory advancement  
- Universal health equity implementation

The Board shall periodically review and adapt these phases based on organizational progress, emerging opportunities, and evolving global health priorities.

### Section 1.3: Registered Office

Harvard Business Services, Inc., 16192 Coastal Highway, Lewes, Delaware 19958\.

## ARTICLE II: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### Section 2.1: Board Authority and Composition

**(a) Authority:** The Foundation has no members within the meaning of Delaware General Corporation Law. The Board of Directors exercises all corporate powers and manages all affairs of the Foundation.

**(b) Size:** The Board shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than twenty-five (25) directors, with the exact number determined by Board resolution based on strategic needs.

**(c) Expertise:** The Board shall maintain diverse expertise appropriate to current operational needs, including but not limited to: technology, healthcare, research, business, finance, law, policy, and global health.

**(d) Diversity Commitment:** The Board shall actively seek diversity in expertise, background, geography, gender, race, ethnicity, and lived experience to ensure comprehensive perspectives in advancing global health equity.

### Section 2.2: Initial Directors

- **Dr. Shahin Mohammadi (Chair)** \- AI/computational health sciences  
- **Dr. Ananth Grama (Vice Chair)** \- Physical AI and academic research  
- **Dr. Madhvi Menon (Chief Communications Officer)** \- Immunology and biomedical research

### Section 2.3: Operations

**(a) Meetings:** Quarterly minimum, with additional meetings as necessary. Virtual participation is enabled for international coordination and accessibility.

**(b) Quorum:** A majority of directors then in office shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

**(c) Independence:** At least fifty-one percent (51%) of directors must be independent, having no financial interest in Foundation subsidiaries or material business relationships with the Foundation.

**(d) Terms:** Directors serve three-year staggered terms to ensure continuity, with no limit on consecutive terms.

**(e) Removal:** Directors may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds vote of the remaining directors.

## ARTICLE III: OFFICERS

### Section 3.1: Officer Positions

**(a) Required Officers:** The Foundation shall have a Chair, Vice Chair, and Chief Communications Officer.

**(b) Additional Officers:** The Board may appoint additional officers as deemed necessary, including but not limited to a Chief Governance Officer, Treasurer, Chief Executive Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Technology Officer, and Chief Development Officer.

**(c) Secretary Designation:** The Chief Communications Officer serves as the corporate Secretary for purposes of Delaware law, with administrative duties as specified. Legal certification and authentication duties may be performed by the Chair or delegated to external counsel until a Chief Governance Officer is appointed.

### Section 3.2: Officer Appointment and Removal

**(a) Appointment:** All officers shall be appointed by a majority vote of the Board of Directors. The Board may appoint new officers at any regular or special meeting.

**(b) Terms:** Officers serve at the pleasure of the Board with no fixed terms unless otherwise specified by Board resolution.

**(c) Removal:** Any officer may be removed with or without cause by a majority vote of the Board.

**(d) Vacancy:** Officer vacancies may be filled by majority vote of the Board at any time.

### Section 3.3: Officer Duties

**(a) Chair (may also serve as President):**

- Provide strategic leadership and vision alignment  
- Serve as primary external representative and spokesperson  
- Ensure mission consistency across all activities  
- Lead board meetings and strategic planning processes  
- Execute contracts and agreements as authorized by the Board  
- May use the title "President" when executing documents if needed

**(b) Vice Chair (may also serve as Vice President):**

- Provide scientific and technical oversight  
- Support Chair in operational leadership  
- Lead research initiatives and partnerships  
- Assume Chair duties in the absence or incapacity  
- Execute documents as authorized when acting as Chair  
- May use the title "Vice President" when executing documents if needed

**(c) Chief Communications Officer:**

- Manage internal board communications and information flow among members  
- Record, maintain, and distribute meeting minutes  
- Ensure timely distribution of meeting materials and notices  
- Support the administrative functions of the board  
- Coordinate board and committee meeting schedules  
- Serve as a point of contact for board correspondence

**(d) Chief Governance Officer (when appointed):**

- Maintain comprehensive corporate records and ensure regulatory compliance  
- Manage legal documentation, filings, and corporate formalities  
- Oversee governance best practices and board policies  
- Ensure adherence to Delaware corporate law and federal tax requirements  
- Serve as liaison with legal counsel  
- Certify board resolutions and corporate documents as required  
- Coordinate document authentication and legal attestations  
- Guide organizational compliance and regulatory strategy

**(e) Treasurer (when appointed):**

- Provide financial oversight and fiscal management  
- Ensure proper accounting and financial controls  
- Oversee audit processes and financial reporting  
- Monitor compliance with IRS requirements and restrictions  
- Present financial reports to the board  
- Maintain financial records and execute financial documents as authorized  
- Sign tax returns and financial reports as required

**(f) Chief Development Officer (when appointed):**

- Lead organizational communication and outreach strategy  
- Develop strategic partnerships and collaborations  
- Oversee fundraising and resource development  
- Build relationships with donors, investors, and stakeholders  
- Guide external communications and public relations

**(g) Other Officers:** Duties as assigned by the Board through resolution or position descriptions.

## ARTICLE IV: HYBRID STRUCTURE AND CAPPED-PROFIT MODEL

### Section 4.1: Organizational Structure

**(a) Parent Foundation:** The Foundation serves as the parent 501(c)(3) organization, maintaining overall control and ensuring charitable mission alignment.

**(b) Capped-Profit Subsidiaries:** The Foundation may establish capped-profit subsidiaries that:

- Attract necessary capital for scaling impact  
- Provide limited returns to investors based on investment risk and timing  
- Ensure the majority of value creation flows to charitable purposes  
- Maintain mission alignment through governance controls

### Section 4.2: Investment Return Structure

**(a) Return Caps:** Investment returns in capped-profit subsidiaries shall be limited based on:

- **Stage of Investment:** Earlier stage investments may receive higher return multiples (e.g., 10x-100x cap), reflecting higher risk  
- **Investment Timing:** Later investments receive lower multiples (e.g., 2x-10x cap), reflecting reduced risk  
- **Investment Type:** Strategic partnerships may have different cap structures than financial investments

**(b) Cap Determination:** The Board shall establish specific return caps for each funding round, considering:

- Stage of technology development  
- Market and regulatory risks  
- Amount of capital needed  
- Strategic value of investors  
- Precedent transactions in similar structures

**(c) Excess Returns:** All returns exceeding investor caps flow to the Foundation for exclusive use in charitable activities.

### Section 4.3: Governance Controls

**(a) Foundation Control Mechanisms:**

- Majority board representation in all subsidiaries  
- Veto rights over fundamental changes  
- Mission-protective provisions in organizational documents  
- Regular impact assessment and reporting

**(b) Investor Rights Limitations:**

- Capped financial returns as specified  
- Information rights appropriate to investment  
- Limited governance participation without control  
- No ability to force exit or sale, contrary to the mission

### Section 4.4: Revenue Flow Structure

**(a) Operating Revenue:**

- Product sales and licensing (with differential pricing)  
- Service delivery and platform fees  
- Strategic partnerships and collaborations  
- Government contracts and reimbursements

**(b) Philanthropic Revenue:**

- Foundation grants and awards  
- Individual donations and major gifts  
- Corporate philanthropy and sponsorships  
- Endowment income

**(c) Investment Revenue:**

- Capped returns to early investors based on risk profile  
- Program-related investments from foundations  
- Patient capital with below-market returns  
- Social bonds and impact investments

**(d) Distribution Priority:**

1. Operating expenses and reasonable reserves  
2. Capped returns to investors per agreement  
3. Reinvestment in R\&D and technology development  
4. Global access and equity programs  
5. All excess to the Foundation for charitable purposes

## ARTICLE V: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY STRATEGY

### Section 5.1: Ownership and Management

The Foundation and its controlled entities own all intellectual property developed through their activities. Strategic portfolio management shall balance competitive advantage with commitments to open science and global access, always prioritizing public benefit over profit maximization.

### Section 5.2: Licensing Strategy

- **Commercial Markets:** Standard licensing to generate revenue for charitable programs  
- **Middle-Income Markets:** Tiered pricing based on economic indicators  
- **Low-Income Markets:** At-cost or subsidized access  
- **Humanitarian Use:** Royalty-free licensing for approved humanitarian purposes  
- **Research Use:** Open licensing for non-commercial research

### Section 5.3: Revenue Reinvestment

After satisfying capped investor returns, all IP revenue shall be reinvested in research, development, and global health equity activities.

---

## ARTICLE VI: SUBSIDIARY GOVERNANCE

### Section 6.1: Creation Authority

The Foundation may establish wholly-owned or controlled for-profit corporations, limited liability companies, benefit corporations, and capped-profit entities to advance its charitable purposes.

### Section 6.2: Control Structure

**(a) Foundation Control:** Foundation maintains majority voting control of all subsidiaries

**(b) Charitable Purpose:** All subsidiary activities must demonstrably advance charitable purposes and public benefit

**(c) Independent Voting:** Only independent directors (without subsidiary financial interests) may vote on subsidiary-related Foundation decisions

**(d) Profit Distribution:** After capped investor returns, all profits flow to the Foundation for exclusive use in charitable activities

**(e) Impact Assessment:** Annual charitable impact assessment required for all commercial activities

### Section 6.3: Mission Alignment

All subsidiary activities require:

- Clear advancement of charitable purposes  
- Investor return caps protecting charitable assets  
- Pricing strategies ensuring affordability and access  
- Regular impact reporting against health equity goals  
- Prohibition on activities contrary to the charitable mission

---

## ARTICLE VII: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

### Section 7.1: Financial Controls

**(a) Fiscal Year:** Calendar year (January 1 \- December 31\)

**(b) Controls:** Financial controls appropriate to operational complexity, including:

- Segregation of duties and approval hierarchies  
- Regular financial reporting to the Board  
- Compliance with all applicable accounting standards  
- Adherence to IRS regulations for tax-exempt organizations  
- Tracking of capped returns and charitable distributions

**(c) Audits:** Annual independent audits by certified public accountants when gross revenues exceed $750,000, with voluntary audits before this threshold

**(d) Public Disclosure:** Annual filing of Form 990 and public availability as required by law

### Section 7.2: Use of Funds

The Foundation shall use its funds exclusively for charitable purposes, with flexibility to adapt allocations based on strategic phase and operational needs, ensuring maximum impact on global health equity.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: GOVERNANCE SAFEGUARDS

### Section 8.1: Conflicts of Interest

**(a) Policy Requirements:** Written conflict of interest policy requiring:

- Annual disclosure statements from all directors and officers  
- Recusal from discussions and votes on conflicted matters  
- Documentation of all conflict determinations and resolutions  
- Special procedures for capped-profit investment decisions

**(b) Related-Party Transactions:** All related-party transactions must be:

- At or below fair market value  
- Documented with an independent valuation for amounts exceeding $100,000  
- Approved only by disinterested directors  
- Reported in annual Form 990

### Section 8.2: Transparency and Accountability

**(a) Public Disclosure:** Public disclosure of:

- Annual impact reports with quantitative health outcomes  
- Capped-profit structure and investor returns  
- Progress against mission objectives  
- Financial information as required by law

**(b) Stakeholder Engagement:** Stakeholder engagement through:

- Community advisory boards in target populations  
- Regular consultation with beneficiary communities  
- Transparent communication with all stakeholders  
- Responsive grievance mechanisms

### Section 8.3: Mission Protection

**(a) Prohibited Amendments:** No amendment to these Bylaws or subsidiary documents may:

- Eliminate or reduce investor return caps retroactively  
- Transfer control from the Foundation to investors  
- Prioritize profits over charitable mission  
- Enable distribution of charitable assets for private benefit

**(b) Annual Review:** The Board shall annually review and affirm:

- Continued alignment with charitable purposes  
- Appropriate balance between sustainability and impact  
- Effectiveness of the capped-profit structure  
- Progress toward global health equity goals

## ARTICLE IX: COMMITTEES

### Section 9.1: Committee Formation

The Board may establish committees as needed to support governance and operations. Initial committees may include:

- Executive Committee  
- Audit and Finance Committee  
- Investment and Sustainability Committee  
- Scientific Advisory Committee  
- Impact and Equity Committee

### Section 9.2: Committee Authority

Committees serve in an advisory capacity to the Board, with delegated authority as specified in committee charters approved by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE X: INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 10.1: Scope

The Foundation shall indemnify its directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law, including advancement of expenses incurred in defense of proceedings.

### Section 10.2: Insurance

The Foundation shall maintain appropriate directors' and officers' liability insurance with coverage limits commensurate with operational scale and risk profile.

---

## ARTICLE XI: AMENDMENTS

### Section 11.1: Amendment Authority

These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of directors then in office, provided:

- Amendments remain consistent with the Certificate of Incorporation  
- Tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) is preserved  
- Charitable purposes and mission protection are maintained  
- Investor return caps are not increased retroactively  
- Ten (10) days' written notice of proposed amendments is provided

### Section 11.2: Periodic Review

The Board shall conduct comprehensive governance reviews at major organizational milestones to ensure structures optimally support mission and operations.

---

## ARTICLE XII: DISSOLUTION

Upon dissolution, after payment of all liabilities and satisfaction of capped investor returns per existing agreements, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively to one or more organizations:

- Organized and operated exclusively for charitable, scientific, or educational purposes  
- Qualifying under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code  
- Preferably continuing AI-native health technology development for global health equity  
- With demonstrated commitment to serving underserved populations

No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, or private individual beyond contractually obligated capped returns.

---

## ARTICLE XIII: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 13.1: Operations

**(a) Records:** The Foundation maintains accurate books and records appropriate for multi-jurisdictional operations

**(b) Electronic Business:** Business may be conducted through electronic communications to facilitate global coordination

**(c) Nondiscrimination:** The Foundation ensures nondiscrimination in all activities based on race, color, religion, gender, gender expression, age, national origin, disability, marital status, sexual orientation, military status, or economic status

**(d) Equitable Access:** All activities prioritize equitable access regardless of ability to pay or geographic location

### Section 13.2: Prohibited Activities

The Foundation shall not:

- Engage in substantial lobbying activities prohibited under Section 501(c)(3)  
- Participate in political campaigns for or against candidates  
- Engage in activities not permitted for tax-exempt organizations  
- Allow private inurement or impermissible private benefit beyond capped investor returns

### Section 13.3: Adoption

The incorporator adopted these Bylaws on September 8, 2025, subject to ratification by the initial Board of Directors.
