# BYLAWS OF CYTOGNOSIS FOUNDATION, INC.

## A Delaware Nonprofit Corporation

### Delaware File Number: 10324506

### Date of Incorporation: September 8, 2025

### As Amended: September 29, 2025

---

## ARTICLE I: NAME, PURPOSE, AND POWERS

### Section 1.1: Name

The name of this corporation is **Cytognosis Foundation, Inc.** (the "Foundation").

### Section 1.2: Purpose

The Foundation is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, specifically to:

- Advance AI-native technologies for disease detection, monitoring, and prevention  
- Conduct and support biomedical research addressing global health equity  
- Develop accessible diagnostic platforms for underserved populations  
- Foster collaboration between artificial intelligence, computational biology, and clinical medicine  
- Disseminate research findings through open publication and knowledge sharing

### Section 1.3: Powers

The Foundation shall have all powers granted to nonprofit corporations under Delaware law necessary to accomplish its charitable purposes, including the power to conduct research, employ personnel, solicit grants and donations, enter into contracts and partnerships, acquire and dispose of property, and establish subsidiary organizations.

## ARTICLE II: OFFICES

### Section 2.1: Principal Office

The Foundation's principal office shall be located as determined by the Board of Directors, currently in San Carlos, California.

### Section 2.2: Registered Office

The Foundation shall maintain a registered office and agent in Delaware as required by law, currently Harvard Business Services, Inc., 16192 Coastal Highway, Lewes, Delaware 19958\.

## ARTICLE III: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1: General Powers

The Foundation's affairs shall be managed by or under the direction of its Board of Directors, which shall exercise all powers granted to the corporation except as otherwise provided by law or these Bylaws.

### Section 3.2: Number and Composition

The Board shall consist of not fewer than three (3) and not more than fifteen (15) directors as determined by Board resolution. The Board shall maintain appropriate expertise in scientific research, technology development, nonprofit governance, financial management, and the Foundation's mission areas.

### Section 3.3: Election and Term

Directors shall be elected by majority vote of the existing Board and serve three-year terms on a staggered basis to ensure continuity. Directors may be re-elected without limit.

### Section 3.4: Meetings

**Regular Meetings:** The Board shall meet at least quarterly.

**Special Meetings:** May be called by the Chair or any two directors with seven (7) days' notice.

**Quorum and Voting:** Two-thirds (2/3) of directors constitute a quorum. Decisions require majority approval of directors present unless otherwise specified in these Bylaws.

**Remote Participation:** Directors may participate by telephone, video conference, or other electronic means.

### Section 3.5: Vacancies and Removal

Vacancies shall be filled by majority vote of remaining directors. Directors may be removed for cause by two-thirds vote of the Board.

## ARTICLE IV: OFFICERS

### Section 4.1: Board Officers

The Board shall elect from among its members the following officers:

**(a) Chair:** Presides at Board meetings, represents the Foundation publicly, ensures governance compliance, and coordinates Board activities.

**(b) Vice Chair:** Performs Chair duties in their absence and assists with Board leadership.

**(c) Secretary:** Maintains corporate records, prepares meeting minutes, manages document retention, and coordinates conflict of interest disclosures.

**(d) Treasurer:** Oversees financial management, reviews financial reports, chairs Finance Committee (when formed), and ensures proper internal controls.

**(e) Board Scientific Advisor:** Advises on the scientific merit of research programs, reviews major grant proposals, assesses emerging technologies, and builds relationships with the scientific community.

### Section 4.2: Executive Officers

The Board may appoint the following executive officers who serve at the Board's pleasure:

**(a) Chief Executive Officer (CEO):** Manages all operations, leads fundraising and partnerships, ensures regulatory compliance, implements Board policies, and reports to the Board.

**(b) Chief Financial Officer (CFO):** Oversees financial operations, manages audits and tax filings, ensures grant compliance, and develops budgets and financial models.

**(c) Chief Technology Officer (CTO):** Builds sensing platforms and devices, oversees lab operations, manages device regulatory pathways, and leads manufacturing and quality systems.

**(d) Chief Data Officer (CDO):** Develops AI/ML models, manages data infrastructure, oversees computational pipelines, ensures data privacy and security compliance, and leads software development.

**(e) Chief Scientific Officer (CSO):** Validates scientific rigor, ensures biological plausibility, designs and oversees clinical studies, bridges technology to medical understanding, and leads publication strategy.

**(f) Vice President of Strategic Partnerships & Research Development:** Leads grant writing and management, develops strategic partnerships, manages federal grant compliance, and supports business development.

**(g) Other Officers:** As determined by Board resolution.

### Section 4.3: Compensation

Officer compensation shall be established through a fair and transparent process ensuring IRS intermediate sanctions compliance.

**Board Officers:** Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, and Board Scientific Advisor serve without compensation for their Board service.

**Executive Officers:** May receive reasonable compensation as determined by the Board based on:

- Appropriate market research and comparability data  
- Approval by disinterested directors after full disclosure  
- Contemporaneous documentation in Board minutes  
- Annual review by the Compensation Committee (when formed)

**Market Research:** The Foundation maintains comprehensive market research for all positions, documented in the **Comprehensive Market Research Report (2025)**, which establishes compensation benchmarks based on:

- Comparable nonprofit research organizations  
- Geographic market data (Bay Area and Boston)  
- Position-specific salary surveys and industry reports  
- IRS Form 990 public compensation data  
- Professional compensation databases

**Target Positioning:** The Foundation targets the 60th-75th percentile of market compensation for comparable positions, with CEO/founder positions at the 60th percentile to demonstrate nonprofit mission commitment.

**Compensation Committee:** When formed, shall review all executive compensation decisions, conduct annual market benchmarking updates, ensure IRS compliance, and document all decisions with appropriate comparability data.

### Section 4.4: Limitation of Liability

Officers acting within their defined roles shall not be personally liable for Foundation obligations, except in cases of willful misconduct or knowing violation of law.

## ARTICLE V: ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE AND FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS

### Section 5.1: Current Structure

The Foundation operates as a standalone 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation focused on advancing its charitable mission through research, education, and technology development.

### Section 5.2: Future Organizational Development

The Board may consider various organizational structures to maximize mission impact, including:

**(a)** Establishment of subsidiary entities if beneficial for operations or fundraising **(b)** Development of strategic partnerships with other organizations **(c)** Potential future equity or ownership frameworks if organizational structure evolves

### Section 5.3: Framework Development

Any future equity participation framework or organizational restructuring must be:

**(a)** Formally proposed and thoroughly vetted by the Board **(b)** Reviewed by independent legal counsel for tax and regulatory compliance **(c)** Properly documented and approved through Board resolution **(d)** Implemented only after ensuring all legal and governance requirements are met

**No Current Commitments:** Until such frameworks are formally adopted by the Board, no commitments, promises, or obligations regarding equity participation or ownership interests shall be made to any individual.

---

## ARTICLE VI: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY STRATEGY

### Section 6.1: Ownership and Management

The Foundation and its controlled entities own all intellectual property developed through their activities. Strategic portfolio management shall balance competitive advantage with commitments to open science and global access, always prioritizing public benefit over profit maximization.

### Section 6.2: Licensing Strategy

The Foundation shall implement a licensing strategy that ensures sustainable revenue generation while maintaining global access commitments. Specific licensing terms shall be established by Board resolution and may include:

**(a) Core Platform Technology:** Licensing approaches that maximize innovation and ecosystem development while enabling broad access for research and humanitarian applications.

**(b) Commercial Applications:** Licensing terms for specific products, devices, and validated clinical applications that generate revenue to support the Foundation's mission.

**(c) Humanitarian Applications:** Free or at-cost licensing for technologies addressing neglected diseases, emergency response, and underserved populations.

### Section 6.3: Revenue Reinvestment

All intellectual property revenue shall be reinvested in research, development, and global health equity activities in furtherance of the Foundation's charitable purposes.

---

## ARTICLE VII: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

### Section 7.1: Fiscal Year

The Foundation's fiscal year shall be the calendar year unless changed by Board resolution.

### Section 7.2: Financial Oversight

The Board shall ensure appropriate financial oversight through regular financial reporting, annual budgets, internal controls, and independent audits as required by law or funding sources.

### Section 7.3: Financial Strategy and Controls

The Board shall establish appropriate financial controls including:

**(a) Approval Authorities:** Documented approval authorities for contracts, expenditures, and banking transactions **(b) Budget Management:** Annual budget approval and monitoring of expenditures **(c) Internal Controls:** Segregation of duties, dual signatures for large transactions, and regular reconciliations **(d) Audit Requirements:** Independent audits when required by law, funders, or as determined by the Board **(e) Public Disclosure:** Annual filing of IRS Form 990 and public availability as required by law

The Board may adopt a separate Financial Strategy Document detailing specific compensation structures, approval authorities, budget guidelines, and other financial management policies. This document may be updated by Board resolution without amending these Bylaws.

### Section 7.4: Contracts and Banking

Authority to execute contracts, manage banking relationships, and approve expenditures shall be documented in Board resolutions and operational policies, with appropriate limits and controls to ensure proper financial stewardship.

### Section 7.5: Grants and Funding

The CEO is authorized to submit grant applications consistent with the Foundation's mission and strategic plan. Major grant awards and significant funding relationships shall be reported to the Board.

### Section 7.6: Reserves

The Foundation shall maintain operating reserves consistent with sound nonprofit financial management practices as recommended by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: COMMITTEES

### Section 8.1: Committee Authority

The Board may establish committees to support governance and operations, with composition, authority, and responsibilities specified in committee charters approved by the Board.

### Section 8.2: Committee Formation

The Board may establish committees as needed to support Foundation operations and governance. Committees shall evolve with organizational phases and may include:

**(a) Compensation Committee:** Reviews and recommends executive compensation, conducts market research, oversees performance evaluations, and ensures IRS compliance.

**(b) Grant Management Committee:** Coordinates grant applications, monitors compliance, manages funder relationships, and oversees the Foundation's funding portfolio.

**(c) Scientific Advisory Committee:** Provides expert guidance on research direction, technology development, scientific strategy, and partnerships.

**(d) Other Committees:** Finance/Audit, Investment, Regulatory, Global Access, and Impact Assessment Committees may be established as the organization grows.

### Section 8.3: Committee Operations

Committee composition, responsibilities, meeting requirements, and reporting obligations shall be specified in committee charters approved by the Board. All committees serve in an advisory capacity unless specifically delegated authority by Board resolution.

---

## ARTICLE IX: GOVERNANCE SAFEGUARDS

### Section 9.1: Conflict of Interest

The Board shall adopt and maintain a Conflict of Interest Policy requiring annual disclosure of potential conflicts by all directors, officers, and key employees. Any person with a conflict of interest in a matter before the Board must disclose the conflict and recuse themselves from discussion and voting on that matter.

### Section 9.2: Whistleblower Protection

The Foundation shall adopt a Whistleblower Protection Policy to enable reporting of concerns about financial impropriety, legal violations, or unethical conduct without fear of retaliation.

### Section 9.3: Document Retention

The Foundation shall adopt a Document Retention Policy establishing systematic procedures for retention and destruction of corporate records in compliance with legal requirements.

### Section 9.4: Financial Statement Review

The Board shall review the Foundation's financial statements at least quarterly and shall ensure appropriate audit procedures are conducted annually once the Foundation's revenues exceed applicable thresholds.

---

## ARTICLE X: INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 10.1: Scope

The Foundation shall indemnify its directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law, including advancement of expenses incurred in defense of proceedings.

### Section 10.2: Insurance

The Foundation shall maintain appropriate directors' and officers' liability insurance with limits commensurate with operational scale and risk profile.

---

## ARTICLE XI: SUBSIDIARY GOVERNANCE

### Section 11.1: Creation Authority

The Board of Directors may establish subsidiaries through majority vote, subject to maintaining the Foundation's tax-exempt status and charitable purposes.

### Section 11.2: Control Requirements

If subsidiaries are created:

**(a)** The Foundation shall retain ownership of all grant-funded intellectual property.

**(b)** Subsidiaries shall receive appropriate licenses to Foundation intellectual property.

**(c)** The Foundation shall maintain appropriate governance controls to ensure mission alignment.

**(d)** Any profit distributions from subsidiaries shall flow to the Foundation for use in charitable activities.

---

## ARTICLE XII: AMENDMENTS

### Section 12.1: Amendment Authority

These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of directors then in office, provided:

**(a)** Amendments remain consistent with the Certificate of Incorporation.

**(b)** Tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) is preserved.

**(c)** Charitable purposes and mission protection are maintained.

**(d)** Ten (10) days' written notice of proposed amendments is provided to all directors.

### Section 12.2: Operational Documents

Financial strategy documents, committee charters, and operational policies may be updated by Board resolution without amending these Bylaws, enabling responsive adaptation to organizational growth and changing circumstances.

### Section 12.3: Periodic Review

The Board shall conduct comprehensive governance reviews at major organizational milestones and at least every three (3) years to ensure structures optimally support the Foundation's mission and operations.

---

## ARTICLE XIII: DISSOLUTION

Upon dissolution, after payment of all liabilities, remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively to one or more organizations qualifying under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, preferably those continuing AI-native health technology development for global health equity. No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, or private individual.

---

## ARTICLE XIV: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 14.1: Nondiscrimination

The Foundation shall conduct all activities without discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

### Section 14.2: Electronic Communications

The Foundation may conduct business through electronic communications and remote participation in accordance with Delaware law.

### Section 14.3: Headings

Article and section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect interpretation of these Bylaws.

### Section 14.4: Severability

If any provision of these Bylaws is held invalid or unenforceable, all other provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
