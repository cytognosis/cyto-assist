## **ARTICLE III: PURPOSES AND POWERS**

### **Section 1: Tax-Exempt Purposes**

The Corporation is organized and operated exclusively for **charitable, scientific, and educational purposes** within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), or the corresponding provision of any future United States internal revenue law.

### **Section 2: Specific Purposes**

Without limiting the generality of Section 1, the Corporation is explicitly organized to:

**(a) Advance Scientific Research and Innovation**

- Conduct and support pioneering biomedical research focused on early detection, prevention, and intervention of human disease through AI-native technologies and computational health sciences
- Develop and deploy continuous health monitoring systems, diagnostic technologies, and predictive health analytics that transform healthcare from reactive treatment to proactive prevention
- Map cellular and molecular health states across diverse populations to enable personalized, precision health interventions at the population scale

**(b) Promote Global Health Equity and Access**

- Ensure equitable distribution of health technologies and scientific advances to populations in countries across all economic development levels, with particular emphasis on developing countries as defined by the World Bank based on quantitative metrics such as Gross National Income (GNI) per capita or similar international standards.
- Develop and implement sustainable models for healthcare delivery that remove economic, geographic, and social barriers to health access
- Partner with international health organizations, governments, and communities to build local capacity for health innovation and technology deployment

**(c) Foster Open Science and Knowledge Dissemination**

- Make research findings, datasets, algorithms, and technologies openly accessible following FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) data principles
- Publish scientific discoveries in open-access formats to accelerate global health innovation
- Create educational resources, training programs, and capacity-building initiatives for researchers, healthcare providers, and communities worldwide

**(d) Support Technology Development and Translation**

- Bridge the gap between scientific discovery and real-world health applications through technology transfer and commercialization pathways that prioritize public benefit
- Develop intellectual property strategies that balance innovation incentives with global access requirements
- Support the creation and scaling of health technologies through strategic partnerships, including wholly-owned subsidiaries, when aligned with charitable purposes

**(e) Engage in Health Policy and Systems Change**

- Advocate for policies that promote health equity, scientific advancement, and ethical use of health technologies
- Collaborate with policymakers, regulators, and health systems to create frameworks for responsible innovation in digital health and AI-driven diagnostics
- Support evidence-based health policy development through research and data analysis

### **Section 3: Nondiscrimination and Equity Commitment**

The Corporation affirms that health is a fundamental human right. All activities, programs, and services of the Corporation shall be conducted without discrimination based on race, color, ethnicity, national origin, ancestry, religion, creed, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, physical or mental disability, medical condition, genetic information, marital status, military or veteran status, citizenship status, socioeconomic status, geographic location, or any other characteristic protected by law.

The Corporation commits to actively addressing health disparities and ensuring that its technologies, research, and programs benefit all people, with particular attention to historically marginalized and underserved populations.

## **ARTICLE IV: POWERS AND ACTIVITIES**

### **Section 1: General Powers**

In furtherance of its exempt purposes, the Corporation shall have all powers granted to nonprofit corporations under Delaware law, including but not limited to:

**(a)** Soliciting, receiving, and administering funds from public and private sources, including grants, contributions, and contracts

**(b)** Owning, licensing, and managing intellectual property, including patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets, provided such activities advance the Corporation's charitable purposes

**(c)** Establishing and operating wholly-owned subsidiary corporations, whether nonprofit or for-profit, when such entities advance the Corporation's exempt purposes and are subject to appropriate safeguards

**(d)** Entering into partnerships, joint ventures, and collaborative agreements with other organizations, provided the Corporation maintains control over its charitable assets and activities

**(e)** Conducting clinical research and trials in compliance with all applicable regulations and ethical standards, including but not limited to FDA regulations, international medical device standards, and institutional review board requirements

**(f)** Operating internationally and establishing foreign affiliates or branches as needed to fulfill its global health mission

### **Section 2: Limitations on Activities**

Notwithstanding any other provision herein:

**(a)** No part of the net earnings shall inure to the benefit of any director, officer, or other private person, except that reasonable compensation may be paid for services rendered and payments may be made in furtherance of exempt purposes

**(b)** No substantial part of the Corporation's activities shall consist of carrying on propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, except as permitted under Section 501(h) of the Code

**(c)** The Corporation shall not participate or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office

**(d)** The Corporation shall not engage in any activities not permitted for organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code or organizations to which contributions are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code

---

## **ARTICLE V: NONPROFIT STRUCTURE**

The Corporation is organized on a **nonstock basis** and shall not have authority to issue capital stock. The Corporation shall have **no members** with voting rights. All corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Directors.

---

## **ARTICLE VI: BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

### **Section 1: Composition**

The Board of Directors shall consist of not less than three (3) nor more than twenty-five (25) directors, with the exact number to be fixed by the Bylaws. The Board composition shall reflect diversity in expertise, geography, gender, ethnicity, and other dimensions to ensure global perspectives and equitable governance.

### **Section 2: Initial Board and Appointment**

The initial Board of Directors shall be appointed in accordance with the Bylaws adopted by the incorporator. The incorporator shall have the power to adopt initial Bylaws and appoint the initial Board of Directors, who shall serve until the first annual meeting or until their successors are duly elected and qualified.

### **Section 3: Duties and Standards**

Directors shall discharge their duties in good faith, with the care an ordinarily prudent person would exercise under similar circumstances, and in a manner the director reasonably believes to be in the best interests of the Corporation and its charitable purposes.

---

## **ARTICLE VII: SUBSIDIARY GOVERNANCE**

### **Section 1: Authority to Create Subsidiaries**

The Corporation may create, own, and operate subsidiary entities, including for-profit subsidiaries, when doing so advances the Corporation's charitable purposes. Such subsidiaries may be established for purposes including but not limited to:

- Developing and distributing health technologies at scale
- Generating revenue to support the Corporation's charitable activities
- Creating sustainable models for global health technology deployment
- Facilitating technology transfer and commercialization

### **Section 2: Oversight and Control**

The Board shall maintain appropriate oversight of all subsidiaries through:

- Board representation and control mechanisms
- Regular reporting requirements
- Alignment of subsidiary activities with charitable purposes
- Distribution of subsidiary profits exclusively for charitable purposes
- Arm's length transactions and fair market value standards

**Intellectual Property Management:** The Corporation shall maintain strategic control over key intellectual property developed by or licensed to subsidiaries, ensuring that:

- Core health technologies remain accessible for charitable purposes
- Commercial licensing generates sustainable revenue for the Foundation's mission
- Global access commitments are preserved across all subsidiary activities
- Technology transfer agreements include appropriate charitable use provisions

---

## **ARTICLE VIII: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST**

The Corporation shall adopt and maintain a comprehensive conflict of interest policy applicable to all directors, officers, and key employees. This policy shall require:

- Annual disclosure of financial interests
- Recusal from decisions where conflicts exist
- Documentation of conflict resolution procedures
- Independent review of related party transactions
- Compliance with IRS intermediate sanctions rules

---

## **ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY PROTECTION**

### **Section 1: Limitation of Liability**

To the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law, no director or officer shall be personally liable to the Corporation or its stakeholders for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty, except for:

- Breach of the duty of loyalty
- Acts or omissions not in good faith or involving intentional misconduct
- Unlawful distributions
- Transactions from which an improper personal benefit was derived

### **Section 2: Indemnification**

The Corporation shall indemnify and advance expenses to directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law, including purchasing and maintaining appropriate insurance coverage.

### **Section 3: Preservation of Rights**

Any amendment or repeal of this Article shall not adversely affect rights to indemnification for acts or omissions occurring before such amendment or repeal.

---

## **ARTICLE X: FISCAL PROVISIONS**

### **Section 1: Fiscal Year**

The Board of Directors shall determine the fiscal year.

### **Section 2: Financial Accountability**

The Corporation shall maintain accurate and up-to-date financial records in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles. It shall obtain independent audits as required by law or as recommended by best practices for organizations of comparable size and scope.

### **Section 3: Compensation Philosophy**

Compensation for services shall be reasonable and appropriate, determined through documented processes including independent comparability data and Board review without participation by interested parties.

---

## **ARTICLE XI: DISSOLUTION**

Upon dissolution of the Corporation:

**(a)** All liabilities and obligations shall be paid, satisfied, and discharged, or adequate provision shall be made therefor

**(b)** Assets held under donor restrictions shall be returned or transferred consistent with such restrictions

**(c)** All remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively to one or more organizations:

- Organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes
- Qualifying as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code
- Aligned with the Corporation's mission of advancing global health equity

**(d)** No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, or other private individual

**(e)** Distribution priorities shall favor organizations continuing similar work in underserved communities

---

## **ARTICLE XII: AMENDMENTS**

### **Section 1: Amendment Authority**

This Certificate may be amended in accordance with the Delaware General Corporation Law and the Corporation's Bylaws.

### **Section 2: Limitations**

No amendment shall be made that would:

- Cause the Corporation to cease qualifying under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code
- Permit private inurement or private benefit
- Substantially change the Corporation's charitable purposes without appropriate regulatory approval
- Violate applicable laws or regulations

### **Section 3: Notice Requirements**

Amendments affecting fundamental aspects of the Corporation's structure or purposes shall require advance notice to stakeholders as specified in the Bylaws.

---

## **ARTICLE XIII: PERPETUAL EXISTENCE**

The Corporation shall have **perpetual existence**, unless it is dissolved in accordance with the law and this Certificate of Incorporation.
