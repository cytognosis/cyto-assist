# Effective Altruism - Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF) Application Sections

## 1. What are you trying to do, and why? (Impact and Scale)

Our goal is to build the "Neuroverse," an open-source, on-device AI system that maps and predicts mental health trajectories continuously. Reactive healthcare operates with profound temporal and mechanistic blindspots. Today, mental health care relies on categorical, episodic assessments (PHQ-9) that fail over 50% of the time. The expected value of solving this is enormous: mental health and neurodegenerative ailments are massive contributors to global DALYs (Disability-Adjusted Life Years). By creating an open-source "GPS for Health" focused initially on the brain, we want to replace subjective guessing with continuous, privacy-first biomarker sensing and edge AI (<5 mW). The output is a highly scalable, equitable intervention system that can be deployed at zero marginal cost per software download globally.

## 2. Why is philanthropy (specifically LTFF) the right funding source?

The traditional startup model fundamentally fails early-stage public health infrastructure. To raise Series A/B funding, founders must surrender ~60% of equity to investors, mandating a commercial exit that inevitably prioritizes premium markets over global access (The Dilution vs. Mission Dilemma). LTFF capital acts as non-dilutive R&D philanthropy to cross the "Valley of Death." By funding Cytognosis non-dilutively, that 60% equity remains intact as "The People's Equity" within our 501(c)(3) foundation. This creates a regenerative financial engine: when we commercialize, our foundation's permanent holdings mandate tiered access and fund further open-source science. LTFF's contribution yields exponential downstream QALYs that a standard VC structure would capture for purely financial returns.

## 3. How does this mitigate catastrophic or existential risks?

The Neuroverse project directly mitigates two escalating systemic risks:

1. **Mental Health Cascades**: The collapse of cognitive and emotional resilience at a population scale under the stressors of rapid technological and environmental shifts. Continuous, predictive mental health sensing acts as an early-warning radar for societal well-being.
2. **AI Safety & Privacy Risks**: By deploying highly advanced, purpose-built mental health LLMs *entirely on-device* through federated learning and differential privacy, we provide a robust, open-source blueprint for decentralized AI. It ensures that the most sensitive data humanity possesses—its neurological and psychological states—never sits in centralized corporate clouds, mitigating extreme data misuse and single-point-of-failure vulnerabilities.
