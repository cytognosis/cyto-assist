## Coordinated Research Program Description

### **What specifically are you trying to do?**

We're building a comprehensive health-tracking 

AI-native platform for comprehensive health tracking—continuous glucose monitoring, but for dozens of biomarkers optimized for YOUR genetics and risk factors.

Two integrated technologies:

1. **Cytoverse**: AI mapping single-cell data to create "Google Maps for health."  
2. **Cytoscope**: Programmable spectroscopy sensors detecting cellular dysfunction years before symptoms

Current medicine waits for the fire to spread before calling firefighters. We detect the first spark when a glass of water is enough. Sensors collect longitudinal molecular data while AI learns your unique patterns, enabling intervention before permanent damage.

Output: Platform democratizing health autonomy by giving 40M at-risk Americans 5-10 years to prevent disease instead of treating symptoms.

---

### **What limitations in current knowledge or capabilities will your program address?**

Three critical gaps exist:

1. **Sensor inflexibility**: Today's biosensors are locked to specific molecules via antibodies. Need different markers? Build new sensors—expensive and impractical for personalized monitoring.

2. **Unknown targets**: We don't know which signatures predict disease for different individuals. Same disease manifests through different molecular pathways across genetic backgrounds.

3. **Temporal blindness**: Quarterly blood panels miss dynamic patterns. Disease is a trajectory of cellular conversations changing in minutes—not static thresholds.

Current "solutions" fail: Zoe/CGMs only track glucose. Function Health takes snapshots when we need movies. The recent molecular monitoring FRO builds sensors but doesn't know what to measure for whom. We provide both programmable sensors AND intelligence to personalize them.

### **If your program doesn't succeed, what are the most likely reasons?**

1. **Spectroscopy sensitivity**: May lack picomolar detection for brain proteins. Mitigation: SERS enhancement, photonic resonators

2. **Biological noise**: Individual trajectories too variable for prediction. Mitigation: Start with high-penetrance genetics (APOE4, PSEN1)

3. **Regulatory barriers**: FDA may require traditional endpoints. Mitigation: Partner with FDA Biomarker Qualification Program from day 1

4. **Privacy concerns**: Continuous molecular monitoring raises unprecedented data issues. Mitigation: On-device AI, homomorphic encryption

Key assumption: Disease trajectories are predictable with sufficient molecular resolution—proven in our cell atlases but needs validation at population scale.

## Vision for Impact

This coordinated research program represents the **Manhattan Project for early disease detection**. Just as radar transformed warfare from visual to electronic detection, programmable molecular sensing transforms medicine from symptom-based to predictive.

By creating sensors that adapt to each person's biology and AI that learns what matters for whom, we're not just building better diagnostics—we're creating a new paradigm where disease becomes a preventable trajectory rather than an inevitable destination. The question isn't whether we'll achieve molecular weather forecasting for human health, but whether we'll do it fast enough for the 40 million Americans currently progressing toward Alzheimer's.

Through the BRAINs accelerator, we'll refine this vision into an executable program design, build connections with ARPA-H and major foundations, and assemble the coalition needed to make pre-symptomatic detection the standard of care within this decade.
