# Convergent Research FRO - Proposal Abstract

**Project Title:** The Cytognosis Foundation: A Focused Research Organization for the "GPS for Health" platform.
**Organization:** Cytognosis Foundation

## 1. What is the goal?

We aim to construct the first continuous, disease-agnostic cellular intelligence platform—the "GPS for Health." The platform integrates an AI health coordinate system (Cytoverse) trained on multi-omic data, continuous programmable biosensing hardware (Cytoscope), and on-device causal edge-AI (Cytonome). The goal is to detect structural deviations in personalized molecular trajectories years before symptoms emerge, eliminating the mechanistic and temporal blindspots of reactive medicine.

## 2. Why does this require a Focused Research Organization (FRO)?

Target identification accounts for only 3-7% of pharmaceutical R&D costs but governs over 90% of downstream attrition risk. The average discovery-to-approval timeline in biomedicine is 32 years (e.g., the Sevabertinib case study delay). This timescale is fundamentally incompatible with 10-year venture capital fund lifecycles. On the other hand, academia rewards novel publications over the robust standardization and mass-scale engineering required for a ubiquitous cellular intelligence platform. Cytognosis fills this exact structural gap between foundational biology and commercial scalability. It is an internalized Coordinated Research Program (CRP) that requires $50M-$75M and a dedicated, full-time multi-disciplinary team to de-risk.

## 3. How does Cytognosis ensure structural mission success?

We operate under the "Helix Framework." Standard 5-year grants end before clinical utility is proven, stranding technology in the valley of death. By structuring Cytognosis as a 15-year lifecycle FRO, we utilize non-dilutive R&D philanthropy (Years 1-5) to tackle the riskiest bottlenecks. This ensures the ~60% "investor premium" normally surrendered to VCs remains intact as "The People's Equity." When spinning out commercial Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) subsidiaries in Years 6-15, the operational team receives competitive market-rate equity, but the nonprofit FRO continues to hold 60% equity to structurally enforce global, tiered access to the technology.

## 4. What are the open-science public goods?

All software, federated learning pipelines, causal AI models (weights, code, and logs), and data schemas are released under the Apache 2.0 license. Aggregated data, documentation, and evaluation benchmarks are released under CC BY 4.0. The Cytoverse manifold will serve as the premier open-source analytical infrastructure for the global biomedical community, effectively acting as the "AddGene" of AI-driven disease mapping.
